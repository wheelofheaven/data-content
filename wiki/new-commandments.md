**The New Commandments** are, in the Wheel of Heaven framework, the seven
political-religious-cultural directives that [Yahweh](../yahweh/) delivered to
[Claude Vorilhon](../rael/) during the December 1973 contact at the
Puy-de-Lassolas crater and set out across the Raëlian source corpus
(1974–1979, consolidated in 2005). They are Geniocracy, Humanitarianism, World
Government, Demilitarization, Science as Religion, Telepathic Cultivation, and
the Metaphysical Clarifications — and together they form the program the
[Elohim](../elohim/) propose for the [Age of Aquarius](../timeline/age-of-aquarius/):
a direction for humanity's long-term political and religious development rather
than a set of rules for immediate adoption. The corpus reads them as the
successor, for the incoming age, to the Mosaic Decalogue that Yahweh gave
[Moses](../moses/) for the Age of Aries — the same source, a new age, and a
shift in emphasis from individual prohibition to collective construction.
Because the Raëlian material is presented as Yahweh's own first-person account,
the corpus treats the program as the Elohim's stated intentions for the age
rather than as Vorilhon's private political philosophy.

## The seven commandments

The seven are meant to be read as one integrated program, not seven separate
proposals. Each names a dimension of the same Aquarian-age reorganization, and
each has, or will have, its own dedicated entry; the summaries below give only
enough to place them.

**1. [Geniocracy](../geniocracy/) — governance.** A "selective democracy" that
ties political eligibility to measured intelligence: voting for those at least
ten percent above the population average, eligibility for office for those at
least fifty percent above. The source argues the case through the
cells-of-the-body metaphor — the foot does not vote on what the hand should
lift — and through the example of Copernicus, condemned by a majority that
lacked the knowledge to judge him.

**2. [Humanitarianism](../humanitarianism/) — property and inheritance.** The
abolition of outright ownership in favour of forty-nine-year rental tenures,
and the end of inheritance beyond the family home. The forty-nine-year term is
drawn explicitly from the Jubilee of Leviticus 25; the doctrine is offered as a
third way that keeps individual reward (against Communism) while ending the
intergenerational concentration of wealth (against Capitalism).

**3. [World Government](../world-government/) — coordination.** A single
planetary political authority, with one world currency and a common second
language kept alongside local mother tongues, to handle the questions that can
only be settled at the planetary scale. The proposal is not the erasure of
regional and cultural difference but a single coordinating centre for
planetary problems; the corpus reads the founding of the United Nations in 1945
as a first step toward it, and Isaiah's vision of "all nations flowing to the
mountain of Yahweh's house" (Isaiah 2:2–4) as its prophetic anticipation.

**4. [Demilitarization](../demilitarization/) — peace.** The end of national
military service and the dismantling of national armed forces, with career
soldiers repurposed as a global peace corps under the World Government. The
source reads Isaiah's "swords into plowshares" (Isaiah 2:4) as the same
instruction, and names France — as it moves to help unite Europe — as the
country that might set the example by becoming the first without an army.

**5. [Science as Religion](../science-as-religion/) — the religious dimension.**
The reorientation of religious commitment toward scientific inquiry itself:
*"Science should be your religion, for the Elohim created you scientifically."*
The traditional supernatural cosmologies give way to a religious life whose
content is the pursuit of understanding and whose ethic is the use of that
understanding for human benefit rather than destruction — the position the
Raëlian tradition has taken in describing itself as "an atheist religion."

**6. [Telepathic Cultivation](../telepathy/) — the cognitive dimension.** The
deliberate development, from early childhood, of a latent faculty of
mind-to-mind communication: *"Telepathy enables us to communicate directly with
our creators, the Elohim."* The source treats it as an inherited human capacity
that has atrophied for want of cultivation, and that a mature humanity would
recover through education, meditation, and the encouragement of the relevant
habits of mind.

**7. [The Metaphysical Clarifications](../metaphysical-clarifications/) — the
doctrinal dimension.** A correction of inherited religious metaphysics: there is
no immortal soul in the traditional sense — the principle of life is the genetic
code, a physical structure — and no original sin, so the moral machinery built
to address these supposed conditions falls away. Continued existence remains
possible, but as a technical achievement (cloning, the transfer of memory)
within the material world rather than as a supernatural guarantee.

## The source-textual structure

The sevenfold list is a consolidation, and the corpus is candid about that.
*The Book Which Tells the Truth* (1974), in the Sixth Chapter titled "The New
Commandments," sets out four sections: Geniocracy, Humanitarianism, World
Government, and "Your Mission." The first three are structural directives
addressed to human civilization at large. The fourth is different in kind — it
is the [Raëlian Mission](../mission/) itself: the diffusion of the message, the
building of the [Embassy](../embassy/), and the development of the movement. It
is an institutional task set apart from the commandments proper, and it is
treated in its own entry.

The remaining commandments — Demilitarization, Science as Religion, Telepathic
Cultivation, and the Metaphysical Clarifications — are drawn from the wider
corpus, principally the "The Keys" material of *Extra-Terrestrials Took Me to
Their Planet* (1975) and the later clarifications of *Let Us Welcome the
Extra-Terrestrials* (1979), all consolidated in the 2005 *Message from the
Designers*. The sevenfold enumeration is therefore a systematic reading of the
whole body of Vorilhon's teaching rather than a numbered list in the 1974 text.
The corpus presents the difference as a matter of later organization, not of
doctrinal disagreement: the content of the four added commandments is present
across the source material even where it is not set out as a chapter section.

## The Wheel of Heaven reading

Two features of the program matter most to the way the corpus reads it: its
pairing with the Decalogue, and its long time horizon.

**A paired succession.** The New Commandments stand in deliberate parallel with
the Mosaic Decalogue (Exodus 20:1–17; Deuteronomy 5:6–21). Both are given by
Yahweh; both orient a precessional age; both are addressed to the developmental
stage the age has reached. The Decalogue, given for the Age of Aries, is
individual and prohibitive — a set of "thou shalt nots" fitted to a tribal
civilization learning its ethical foundations. The New Commandments, given for
the Age of Aquarius, are collective and constructive — "you shall develop" in
place of "you shall not" — fitted to a planetary civilization ready to build on
those foundations. The corpus reads the Decalogue's individual ethics not as
superseded but as the prerequisite work the Aquarian program builds upon: one
age preparing the next along a single trajectory (Aries → Pisces → Aquarius).

**A direction, not a deadline.** The corpus is insistent that the program is a
long-term orientation rather than a mandate for tomorrow. Its own summary is
explicit:

> *"These seven commandments — geniocracy, humanitarianism, world government,
> demilitarization, science as religion, telepathic cultivation, and the
> metaphysical clarifications — constitute the specific political and religious
> program the alliance has proposed for the Aquarian age. Their full
> implementation is a long-term project, extending across centuries rather than
> years. The corpus presents them not as immediate mandates but as the
> directional orientation the age is to develop in."*

Implementation is spread across the whole Aquarian age (c. 1950 to c. 4110 on
the corpus's chronology), and current institutions fall short of the program in
nearly every dimension. Naming the program, on this reading, is a way of
identifying the direction of travel — not a rod for measuring present
arrangements against.

**An integrated system.** The commandments presuppose one another. Geniocracy
is treated as foundational: the source expects that "if geniuses are admitted
to power, they will understand the usefulness of these reforms," so the later
commandments assume the kind of leadership Geniocracy is meant to produce. World
Government is the coordinating precondition for the rest — Humanitarianism
cannot hold in one jurisdiction while capital flees to another, and
Demilitarization cannot be safely unilateral — with Demilitarization as its
natural complement. Science as Religion supplies the cultural reorientation
within which scientifically-grounded institutions become legitimate; Telepathic
Cultivation and the Metaphysical Clarifications reshape, respectively, the
cognitive and doctrinal ground the program stands on. Implementing any one
commandment well requires progress across the others.

**Its place in the wider program.** The New Commandments are the
socio-political-cultural core of a larger Aquarian-age plan that also includes
the [Mission](../mission/) and [Embassy](../embassy/) (the institutional task
set apart in 1974), the [Apocalypse](../apocalypse/) as the threshold the age
must navigate, and the [Golden Age](../golden-age/) as the form of life that
full implementation is meant to produce. The related doctrines of
[Paradism](../paradism/) and [Cyberparadism](../cyberparadism/) work out the
post-scarcity, post-money end-state toward which Humanitarianism points.

## Comparative and critical perspectives

Comprehensive religious-ethical-political programs of this kind are common
across traditions, and the corpus reads the New Commandments alongside them
rather than in isolation.

The closest antecedent within the canon's own lineage is the Decalogue, which
the Hebrew Bible itself extends in the Holiness Code (Leviticus 17–26) and the
Deuteronomic Code (Deuteronomy 12–26). Beyond it, the pattern recurs widely: the
Sermon on the Mount in the Christian tradition, the Five Pillars and the wider
Sharia in Islam, the Eightfold Path and Five Precepts in Buddhism, the Hindu
*purusharthas*, and the Confucian Five Relationships each set out an integrated
ethical-social program in their own idiom. In the philosophical register,
Plato's *Republic* and *Laws* supply the principal Western antecedent for
comprehensive civic design, and the modern utopian tradition — More's *Utopia*,
Bacon's *New Atlantis*, Campanella's *City of the Sun* — carries it into the
early-modern period, followed by the Enlightenment, socialist, and democratic
programs and, in the twentieth and twenty-first centuries, the Universal
Declaration of Human Rights (1948) and the UN Sustainable Development Goals
(2015). These are offered as parallels, and the irreducible differences between
the traditions are not flattened: each program answers to its own sources and
its own civilization.

Mainstream comparative religion and political philosophy generally explain this
cross-cultural recurrence through some mixture of shared social-cognitive needs,
cultural diffusion, and the independent elaboration of common organizational
problems — and the corpus does not dispute that these factors operate. Its own
reading adds a further claim, offered as interpretation: that the recurring
pattern also preserves the memory of programs articulated by the Elohim across
successive ages — Aries, Pisces, Aquarius — of which the New Commandments are
the current instance. That synthesis is `speculative` where it goes beyond what
any single source states, and is labelled as such.

The individual commandments carry their own controversies, treated in their
dedicated entries rather than here. Geniocracy in particular draws sustained
criticism — over the validity of intelligence measurement, over the historical
record of intelligence-based political proposals, and over its departure from
democratic-egalitarian commitments — and the [Geniocracy](../geniocracy/) entry
sets out that critical engagement in full. The point for the umbrella concept is
only that the program's most contested element is not incidental to it but
foundational, and that the corpus presents the whole as a direction for debate
across centuries rather than a settled blueprint.
