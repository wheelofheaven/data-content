**Fractal Cosmology** is the framework concept developed in the Wheel of Heaven corpus to articulate the spatial-self-similar structure of the cosmos across the infinite hierarchy of scales. The framework holds that **the cosmos exhibits self-similarity across scales, with the same fundamental patterns of organization recurring at every scale of examination, indefinitely in both directions without finding any bottom or any top**. The framework's principal source-material warrant is the Yahweh-delivered passages in *Extra-Terrestrials Took Me to Their Planet* (1975) and the subsequent Raëlian source material, with the corpus's specific use of *fractal cosmology* as the technical designation registering the structural alignment with the mathematical-fractal vocabulary that Benoît Mandelbrot was developing in the same period the source material was being delivered. The framework operates as the **spatial dimension** of the broader Infinity framework, with the **{% wiki(slug="mass-effect") %}Mass Effect{% end %}** framework operating as the corresponding **temporal dimension** — the two frameworks together constituting the principal cosmological content of the corpus's broader infinite-hierarchical cosmology.

The framework's specific image is the source's striking formulation: *"The Earth is but a particle of the atom of the atoms of the hand of a gigantic being, who contemplates a starlit sky which composes the hand, the stomach or the foot of a being even more gigantic, who finds himself under a sky, etc., etc., ad infinitum."* The universe we observe — the galaxies that fill the night sky, the cosmic microwave background that bounds our observable universe at its furthest reach — is, on this framework, a fragment of a single atom in the body of a vastly larger being, whose own world we cannot perceive. That larger being is in turn one figure standing in a still vaster world that contains uncounted such beings, each composed of countless atoms each containing countless universes like ours. The same pattern extends in the other direction: the atoms that compose our own bodies contain, on the source's claim, beings whose worlds we cannot perceive, who in turn contain still smaller worlds, all the way down without bottom. The framework registers this image not as poetic metaphor but as substantive cosmological description: the cosmos is structurally fractal, with the same fundamental patterns of organization (gravitational structure, particle and energy interactions, the emergence of life and consciousness within suitable environments) recurring at scales that differ from one another by factors of staggering magnitude.

The framework's relationship to mainstream cosmology is one of **substantial structural divergence with specific points of contact**. Mainstream Big Bang cosmology operates through the **cosmological principle** — the assumption that the universe is homogeneous and isotropic at sufficiently large scales, with no preferred locations or directions. The Fractal Cosmology framework rejects this principle: rather than approaching uniform-and-isotropic distribution at large scales, the cosmos approaches further hierarchical self-similar organization at large scales, with each scale revealing further structure rather than smoothing into uniformity. The mainstream cosmological-principle assumption has been substantially contested within contemporary cosmological scholarship: Luciano Pietronero's research from the 1980s onward, the Coleman-Pietronero work on galaxy-distribution analysis, Yurij Baryshev's broader work on hierarchical cosmology, Laurent Nottale's scale-relativity framework, and various other contemporary contributions have produced substantial scholarly literature arguing for fractal or hierarchical cosmological structure as alternatives to or extensions of the standard cosmological model. The framework operates within the contested-fractal-cosmology side of this contemporary scholarly debate while extending substantially beyond what mainstream contested-fractal-cosmology has established through the broader cosmological-hierarchical formulation including consciousness, life, and the broader cosmic-civilizational content.

The reading is interpretive construction. The Raëlian source material provides explicit articulation of the infinite-hierarchical structure, with the principal Yahweh passages establishing the framework's specific content. The corpus's specific use of *fractal cosmology* as technical designation is a retrospective application of subsequently-developed mathematical vocabulary — the term *fractal* was coined by Benoît Mandelbrot in 1975 in *Les objets fractals: forme, hasard et dimension*, after Raël's December 13, 1973 first contact but contemporaneous with the October 7, 1975 second contact at Roc Plat — to source-material content that predates that vocabulary's general availability. The structural alignment between the source's articulation and the mathematical-fractal vocabulary is substantial: the source's specific image (the indefinite hierarchical recursion, the same patterns appearing at every scale, the absence of any findable top or bottom) is precisely the structural pattern that the mathematical-fractal vocabulary identifies. The corpus's framework adopts the technical designation while registering the historical specifics of the timing and the mathematical-historical context.

## Etymology and naming

The framework concept has several distinct designations operating across the source material and the corpus's broader treatment.

The English term **"fractal cosmology"** is the corpus's principal designation for the framework. The corpus's specific use of *fractal cosmology* as technical designation is the retrospective application of subsequently-developed mathematical vocabulary to the source-material content.

### Mathematical-historical context: Mandelbrot and the term *fractal*

The mathematical-historical context is operationally significant. The term *fractal* was coined by Benoît Mandelbrot in 1975 in his book *Les objets fractals: forme, hasard et dimension* (Flammarion, Paris). The term was constructed from the Latin *fractus* (broken, fragmented), with Mandelbrot's specific intent being to identify a category of mathematical structures characterized by self-similar repetition across scales — structures such as the Mandelbrot set itself (whose specific mathematical structure Mandelbrot would not develop until 1980), the Cantor set, the Koch snowflake, and various natural-world objects (coastlines, mountain ranges, lung-bronchial structures, river networks) that exhibit similar self-similar properties.

The chronological alignment with the Raëlian source material is operationally striking:

- **December 13, 1973**: Raël's first contact at Puy-de-Lassolas — *Le Livre qui dit la vérité* dictated; principal initial framework content delivered
- **1975**: Mandelbrot coins the term *fractal* in *Les objets fractals*
- **October 7, 1975**: Raël's second contact at Roc Plat — *Les Extra-Terrestres m'ont emmené sur leur planète* dictated, with the principal infinite-hierarchical passages including the "Earth as particle of an atom of an atom" image and the broader Fractal Cosmology content
- **1980-1982**: Mandelbrot develops the Mandelbrot set and publishes *The Fractal Geometry of Nature* (W. H. Freeman, 1982), the principal popular-scientific articulation of the fractal-mathematical framework
- **1980s**: Popular awareness of fractals develops substantially, with the Mandelbrot set becoming one of the most culturally visible mathematical objects of the late twentieth century

The framework's specific situation: the source-material teaching of October 1975 preserves cosmological content that the mathematical vocabulary subsequently developed across the 1975-1982 period would precisely articulate. The corpus's framework registers this alignment as substantive evidence for the broader cosmological framework's coherence with subsequently-developed mathematical understanding, while not requiring the source material to have used the specific mathematical vocabulary.

## Conventional understanding

The Fractal Cosmology framework as a specific cosmological position has substantial overlap with various contemporary mainstream-scholarly positions, while extending substantially beyond what mainstream science has established.

### Mainstream Big Bang cosmology

The mainstream cosmological framework operates through several specific assumptions that the Fractal Cosmology framework engages substantially.

**The cosmological principle**. The mainstream cosmological framework assumes that the universe is **homogeneous and isotropic** at sufficiently large scales — that there are no preferred locations (homogeneity) and no preferred directions (isotropy) when the universe is examined at scales above some specific threshold. The cosmological principle is operationally significant for the broader mainstream cosmological framework: it permits the use of the Friedmann-Lemaître-Robertson-Walker (FLRW) metric in general-relativistic cosmology, it grounds the standard ΛCDM (Lambda Cold Dark Matter) cosmological model, and it provides the basis for the broader Big Bang cosmological framework.

**The Big Bang event**. Mainstream cosmology dates the Big Bang event to approximately 13.8 billion years before the present, based on various lines of empirical evidence (cosmic microwave background measurements, large-scale-structure observations, primordial nucleosynthesis calculations, various other lines of evidence). The Big Bang on the mainstream framework is the specific event that initiated the observable universe's expansion, with the universe before the Big Bang being either non-existent (on certain interpretations) or in some pre-Big-Bang state (on various other interpretations).

**The observable universe boundary**. Mainstream cosmology recognizes a specific observable universe boundary — the cosmic horizon set by the speed of light and the time elapsed since the Big Bang. The observable universe is approximately 93 billion light-years in diameter (with the apparent paradox between the 13.8-billion-year age and the 93-billion-light-year diameter resolved by the universe's expansion across that period). What lies beyond the observable universe boundary is, on the mainstream framework, undecidable from within our specific observational position.

### The framework's relationship to mainstream cosmology

The Fractal Cosmology framework is **substantially distinct** from each of the principal mainstream-cosmological positions:

**Rejection of the cosmological principle**. The Fractal Cosmology framework rejects the assumption that the universe is homogeneous and isotropic at sufficiently large scales. Rather than approaching uniform-and-isotropic distribution at large scales, the cosmos approaches further hierarchical self-similar organization at large scales, with each scale revealing further structure rather than smoothing into uniformity. The mainstream cosmological-principle assumption is, on the framework's reading, an empirically contestable claim rather than a foundational principle — and various contemporary fractal-cosmology proposals have substantially contested it (treated below under *Modern reinterpretations*).

**Reframing the Big Bang event**. The Fractal Cosmology framework treats the Big Bang as **at most the beginning of our specific observable region** rather than the beginning of being itself. On the framework's reading, the Big Bang is analogous to the formation of a specific cell within the body of one of the larger beings that contains us — a real event with specific causal antecedents and specific causal consequences within its specific context, but not the absolute beginning of existence. The broader cosmic structure within which our specific observable region operates extends substantially beyond what the Big Bang framework addresses.

**Reframing the observable universe boundary**. The Fractal Cosmology framework treats the observable universe boundary as **the boundary of our current observation** rather than the boundary of being itself. On the framework's reading, the cosmic microwave background and the broader observable-universe boundary mark the specific limits of what we can currently observe — limits set by the speed of light and the time elapsed since whatever local event allowed us to begin observing — but not the limits of existence. The broader cosmic structure beyond our specific observation extends indefinitely.

The framework's relationship to mainstream cosmology is therefore one of **substantial structural divergence with specific points of contact**. The framework accepts that mainstream cosmological observations (cosmic microwave background, large-scale-structure surveys, primordial nucleosynthesis) capture genuine empirical phenomena within their specific empirical domain, while extending the framework's broader reading substantially beyond what mainstream cosmology has established.

### Mainstream multiverse proposals

Various mainstream cosmological proposals have produced substantial work on multi-universe frameworks across the past several decades.

**Eternal inflation multiverse** (Linde, Vilenkin, others) proposes that inflation produces an infinite collection of pocket universes, each with potentially different physical constants and laws. The framework provides one specific multi-universe structure within mainstream cosmological theory.

**String-theory landscape** (Susskind, others) proposes that the various solutions to string theory produce an enormous collection of possible universes (approximately 10^500 or more), each with specific physical properties. The framework provides another specific multi-universe structure within theoretical-physics frameworks.

**Many-worlds quantum mechanics** (Everett interpretation) proposes that quantum measurement events produce branching universes, with each measurement outcome being realized in a separate branch. The framework provides a specific multi-universe structure within quantum-mechanical interpretation.

**Cyclic cosmologies** (various proposals including Penrose's conformal cyclic cosmology, Steinhardt-Turok cyclic models) propose that the universe undergoes repeated cycles of expansion-and-collapse or similar structures. The framework provides specific cyclic alternatives to the standard Big Bang framework.

The Fractal Cosmology framework is **distinct from each of these mainstream-multiverse proposals**:

- The framework's structure is **hierarchical-self-similar** rather than parallel-multiverse — the various scales contain each other rather than existing in parallel
- The framework's recursion is **indefinite** rather than finite — there is no specific cosmic structure within which the various scales are contained, because the broader cosmic structure itself extends indefinitely
- The framework's specific cosmological content (the same physical patterns recurring at every scale, the emergence of life and consciousness within suitable environments at every scale) is distinct from the various mainstream-multiverse proposals' specific content

The framework's relationship to mainstream multiverse proposals is one of **categorical distinctness** — the framework operates within a structurally different cosmological category rather than as a variant of the mainstream multiverse frameworks.

### Mainstream science's empirical engagement with self-similarity

Mainstream science has produced substantial empirical and theoretical work on self-similarity and fractal structure across multiple domains.

**Large-scale-structure cosmology**. Various empirical studies of galaxy distribution have produced substantial evidence of fractal-like structure at intermediate scales, with the question of whether this fractal structure extends to the largest cosmological scales (or transitions to homogeneity-and-isotropy at some specific scale threshold) being substantially contested.

**Allometric scaling in biology**. The various allometric scaling laws (Kleiber's law, the West-Brown-Enquist scaling theory, various others) demonstrate substantial scale-invariance in biological organization across substantial portions of the animal kingdom.

**Geological and atmospheric self-similarity**. Various geological and atmospheric phenomena (coastlines, mountain ranges, river networks, cloud structures, lightning patterns) exhibit substantial fractal-mathematical structure, with various specific quantitative analyses producing well-established fractal dimensions for the various phenomena.

**Network theory and complex systems**. Various complex systems exhibit scale-invariant network structure, with substantial scholarly literature on scale-free networks (Barabási, Albert, others) and broader complex-systems theory.

The framework's relationship to mainstream-science empirical work on self-similarity is one of **substantial empirical confirmation within specific domains** — the framework's broader cosmological content extends beyond what mainstream science has established, while the framework's specific structural content is consistent with extensive empirical work on self-similarity in various domains.

> *"From there, we have discovered that the stars and planets are the atoms of a gigantic being, which itself certainly contemplates other stars with curiosity. It is also highly likely that the people living on the infinitely small levels of the infinitely large person and his fellow creatures have known periods when they believed in an immaterial 'God'."*

The passage establishes the principal framework content:

**1. Intelligent life at every scale**. The source's specific claim is that intelligent life exists at every scale, from the level of the infinitely small (the atoms of our atoms) through human scale through the larger scales (the gigantic being of whom the sun is an atom). The intelligent life at every scale is comparable in evolution and complexity to what we are ourselves at our specific scale.

**2. The infinite-hierarchical structure**. The cosmos has an indefinite hierarchical structure, with each scale containing within it the next scale down and being contained within the next scale up. The structure has no findable bottom or top.

**3. The "everything is in everything" principle**. *"You must fully understand that everything is in everything. At this moment in an atom of your arm, millions of worlds are being born and others are dying."* The source's specific principle: each scale of cosmic organization contains within it intelligent civilizations operating at the next scale down.

**4. The cross-cultural recurrence of the cosmological situation**. *"It is also highly likely that the people living on the infinitely small levels of the infinitely large person and his fellow creatures have known periods when they believed in an immaterial 'God'."* The source's specific framing: the cosmological situation that produced the various religious traditions on Earth has presumably produced similar religious traditions at the various scales above and below ours, with each scale's intelligent civilization developing its own religious content responsive to its specific scale-position within the broader infinite hierarchy.

### The mini-world thought experiment

The same FAQ section in *Let's Welcome the Extra-terrestrials* develops a substantial thought experiment elaborating the framework's specific content:

> *"Science is always progressing, and the inhabitants of our phalanx are now at the level to launch always more audacious space explorations. They finally reach a new frontier, the bone of which their planet is but an atom of an atom. This way they can be sure that the universe measures so much by so much. The proof is that, after that point, there is nothing more to be observed."*

> *"A little later they succeed in crossing the immensity which separates the bone from the muscle, and their universe gains new dimensions again."*

> *"They improve their space-ships and finally reach the layer of skin which covers our finger. They have reached the end of their universe, which measures one-and-a-half centimeters by our scale, but was many light-years for them."*

The thought experiment's specific operational content:

**1. The scale-relative perception of cosmic distances**. What measures 1.5 centimeters at our scale measures many light-years at the scale of beings on the atoms of our finger. The cosmic distances perceived by beings at different scales reflect the scale-relative perception of physical extent.

**2. The provisional finality of observed cosmic boundaries**. The mini-world's scientists at each successive stage believe they have reached the boundary of their universe, only to discover that what they had taken to be the boundary is in fact the boundary of one specific structural layer (the bone, the muscle, the skin) within the larger structure of which their universe is one component. **3. The recursive applicability of the principle**. Just as the mini-world's scientists eventually discover that their universe is one component of a larger structure, our scientists have presumably reached or will eventually reach the analogous boundary in our own observation — what we currently take as the cosmic boundary may be the boundary of our specific observation rather than the boundary of being itself.

The conventional theological framework — which generally posits a deity who exists "somewhere" in the cosmos and intervenes "from somewhere" in human affairs — becomes operationally impossible within the broader Fractal Cosmology framework.

## The concept's content

### The basic structural principle

The Fractal Cosmology operates through several interrelated components:

**The basic self-similarity relationship**. The cosmos exhibits self-similarity across scales, with the same fundamental patterns of organization recurring at every scale of examination. Specifically:

- Each scale of cosmic organization contains within it the next scale down
- Each scale is contained within the next scale up
- The pattern continues indefinitely in both directions without finding any bottom or any top
- The same fundamental patterns (gravitational organization, particle and energy interactions, the emergence of life and consciousness within suitable environments) recur at scales that differ from one another by factors of staggering magnitude

**The hierarchical scope**. The relationship operates across the entire cosmological hierarchy:

- **Sub-atomic scales** — the source's specific claim that intelligent life exists on the atoms of our atoms, with their own scale-appropriate cosmologies
- **Atomic and molecular scales** — the substantial empirical context within which our specific physical understanding operates
- **Cellular scales** — the substantial biological context within which our specific living systems operate
- **Human scale** — the reference scale within which the Wheel of Heaven framework operates
- **Planetary scales** — Earth and other planets as living beings operating at their own characteristic scales
- **Stellar and solar-system scales** — the substantial astronomical context within which our specific solar system operates
- **Galactic scales** — the substantial extra-galactic context within which our specific galaxy operates
- **Intergalactic and observable-universe scales** — the substantial cosmological context within which our specific observable region operates
- **Beyond observable-universe scales** — the framework's specific claim that the cosmic structure continues indefinitely beyond our specific observation

**The structural symmetry**. The framework operates symmetrically — the same self-similar pattern continues in both directions across the infinite hierarchy. There is no privileged scale, no specific cosmic center, no specific cosmic boundary. The framework's structural content is inherently symmetric across the infinite-hierarchical organization.

### The specific cosmic-structural content

The framework's specific cosmic-structural content goes beyond mere self-similarity to include substantial operational content:

**The recurrence of physical patterns**. The same fundamental physical patterns recur at every scale: gravitational organization (atoms-and-electrons echoing solar-systems-and-planets, solar-systems echoing galaxies, galaxies echoing intergalactic structure, etc.); particle-and-energy interactions (the basic patterns of attraction-and-repulsion, energy-transfer, structural stability operating across scales); the emergence of complex organization from simpler components (cells from molecules, organisms from cells, civilizations from organisms, planetary systems from stellar systems, etc.).

**The recurrence of biological-and-cognitive patterns**. The framework's specific extension beyond physical patterns: life and consciousness emerge at each scale within suitable environments, with the various scales' biological-cognitive content reflecting the specific physical conditions of those scales while exhibiting the same fundamental patterns. The intelligent civilizations at the various scales develop their own science and self-understanding, each at some point in their history discovering that they are one of many, each eventually approaching the responsibility of contributing to the continuing pattern of life-creation across further scales.

**The recurrence of religious-philosophical patterns**. The framework's specific extension beyond biological-cognitive patterns: the religious and philosophical content that intelligent civilizations develop at various scales presumably exhibits substantial common patterns reflecting the common cosmological situation. The source's specific claim: "It is also highly likely that the people living on the infinitely small levels of the infinitely large person and his fellow creatures have known periods when they believed in an immaterial 'God'." The various scales' civilizations develop religious content responsive to the broader cosmological situation, with the specific religious content varying by cultural-historical context but the broader patterns reflecting the common cosmological situation.

### Mandelbrot and fractal mathematics history

The mathematical history of fractal geometry deserves substantial treatment for the framework's specific terminological context.

**Benoît Mandelbrot** (1924-2010) was the principal mathematical figure responsible for the development of fractal geometry as a recognized mathematical category. Mandelbrot's work across the 1960s-1980s produced the principal mathematical-fractal vocabulary that the corpus's framework uses.

**The 1975 coining of *fractal***. Mandelbrot's *Les objets fractals: forme, hasard et dimension* (Flammarion, Paris, 1975) introduced the term *fractal*. The book provided the initial systematic articulation of the mathematical-fractal framework, drawing on Mandelbrot's prior work with IBM on various irregular-mathematical phenomena (cotton-price fluctuations, telephone-line transmission errors, hydrologic patterns, geographic features). The book introduced the broader fractal-mathematical category but did not yet include the Mandelbrot set itself, which Mandelbrot would develop in 1980.

**The 1980 Mandelbrot set discovery**. Mandelbrot's 1980 work at IBM developed the specific iterative-mathematical structure now known as the Mandelbrot set: the set of complex numbers *c* for which the iteration *z_(n+1) = z_n² + c* (starting from *z₀ = 0*) does not diverge to infinity. The Mandelbrot set's specific visual structure (the principal cardioid with attached circular bulbs, the elaborate boundary structure, the substantial self-similar repetition at every magnification) became one of the most culturally visible mathematical objects of the late twentieth century.

**The 1982 *Fractal Geometry of Nature***. Mandelbrot's *The Fractal Geometry of Nature* (W. H. Freeman, 1982) provided the principal popular-scientific articulation of the fractal-mathematical framework. The book substantially extended the 1975 work, including the Mandelbrot set and substantial additional material on the fractal structure of natural phenomena (coastlines, mountain ranges, lung-bronchial structures, river networks, cloud structures, lightning patterns, various other cases). The book became the principal popular reference for fractal mathematics across the subsequent decades.
