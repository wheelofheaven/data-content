**The Epic of Gilgamesh** is the Standard Babylonian recension of the
Akkadian hero-poem about **Gilgamesh**, king of Uruk, and his
companion **Enkidu**: their adventures, Enkidu's death, and
Gilgamesh's subsequent failed quest for bodily immortality. The
twelve-tablet form, traditionally credited to the scribe Sîn-lēqi-
unninni, was assembled in the later second millennium BCE from
older Sumerian Gilgamesh material. **Tablet XI** carries the
Akkadian Flood narrative — told to Gilgamesh by the immortal flood
survivor **Utnapishtim**, the Akkadian counterpart of Atra-ḫasīs
and of Sumerian Zi-ud-sud-du / Ziusudra.

## About this edition

- **Status:** Scaffold only. Initial focus is **Tablet XI** (the Flood); other tablets will follow.
- **Translation:** R. Campbell Thompson, *The Epic of Gilgamish: A New Translation* (Oxford University Press, 1928), public domain in the United States.
- **Reference format:** `GIL {tablet}:{line}`
- **Original language:** Akkadian (Standard Babylonian)
