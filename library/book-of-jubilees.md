**The Book of Jubilees** (Hebrew: ספר היובלים, *Sefer HaYovelim*)
is a Second Temple Jewish text that retells Genesis and Exodus
through the voice of an angel dictating to Moses on Mount Sinai.

Also called "The Little Genesis," it expands the biblical creation
and patriarchal narratives with precise chronological detail
(organized by jubilee periods of 49 years), an expanded Watchers/
Nephilim narrative closely related to 1 Enoch, and a solar calendar
distinct from the rabbinic lunar calendar.

## About this edition

- **Coverage:** 50 chapters, 686 paragraphs
- **Translation:** R.H. Charles (1902), public domain
- **Source:** pseudepigrapha.com
- **Reference format:** `JUB {chapter}:{paragraph}`

## Why this matters for Wheel of Heaven

Jubilees is the bridge text between Genesis and Enoch. It retells
the same events — creation, Eden, the Watchers, the Flood — but
with the explicit framing that an angel is revealing hidden history.
The creation account in chapter 2 is presented as angelic dictation.
The Watchers narrative in chapter 5 expands Genesis 6:1-4 into a
detailed account of angels descending, teaching forbidden knowledge,
and corrupting the earth — closely paralleling 1 Enoch 6-16.
