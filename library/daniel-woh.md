**Daniel — Wheel of Heaven Translation. Full book (chs 1-12).** The
foundational Hebrew Bible apocalypse + the principal Second Temple
Jewish apocalyptic text. **12 chapters / 357 verses** covering the
six court tales (chs 1-6) and the six apocalyptic visions (chs 7-12).

**Bilingual structure** — the only Hebrew Bible book with intra-book
language transitions: **Hebrew Dan 1:1-2:4a + 8:1-12:13** + **Aramaic
Dan 2:4b-7:28**. The transition occurs within verse 2:4 (marked by
the Hebrew *ʾărāmît* "in Aramaic"); Dan 8:1 returns to Hebrew without
explicit marker. **DSS 4Q112 (the OLDEST Daniel witness, late 2nd c.
BCE per Ulrich et al. DJD XVI 2000) confirms the bilingual structure
was original**, not a late editorial decision. This is the project's
first bilingual single-book translation.

## Narrative arc

**Chs 1-6: The court tales.** The exile to Babylon (ch 1); the
four-metals statue dream + the stone cut without hands (ch 2); the
fiery furnace + the fourth man "like a son of the gods" (ch 3);
Nebuchadnezzar's madness + restoration (ch 4); Belshazzar's feast +
**MENE MENE TEKEL UPHARSIN** (ch 5); the lions' den (ch 6).

**Ch 7: The night vision** — the load-bearing center of the book + of
the entire Second Temple Jewish + early Christian throne-vision
tradition. The four beasts + **the Ancient of Days** (Aramaic
*ʿAttiq Yomin*) seated on a throne of fire + the river of fire +
thousand thousands attending + ten thousand times ten thousand
standing + **books opened** + **one like a son of man** (Aramaic
*kə-bar enash*) coming with clouds of heaven + given dominion + an
eternal kingdom.

**Chs 8-12: The Hebrew apocalyptic visions.** The ram + male goat
vision (ch 8); the **70 weeks** oracle (ch 9); the angelic-warfare
preamble with the seven-attribute angelic figure (ch 10); the
Maccabean apocalypse (ch 11); **the resurrection** + the sealed book +
the time, times, and half a time (ch 12).

## The Daniel 7 throne-vision

Daniel 7:9-14 is the load-bearing center of the Second Temple Jewish
+ early Christian throne-vision tradition. The vision sets up: (i)
THRONES (Aramaic *kursawatâ*, plural); (ii) the ANCIENT OF DAYS
(*ʿAttiq Yomin*) seated on a throne of fire, white-haired as wool;
(iii) the RIVER OF FIRE issuing from before him; (iv) THOUSAND
THOUSANDS + TEN THOUSAND TIMES TEN THOUSAND attending; (v) BOOKS
OPENED for judgment; (vi) ONE LIKE A SON OF MAN approaching with the
clouds of heaven; (vii) ETERNAL DOMINION given.

**This translation completes the four-tradition throne-vision wire
on the translation surface** — Ezekiel 1 ↔ Daniel 7 ↔ 1 Enoch 14 ↔
{% wiki(slug="shiur-qomah") %}Shi'ur Qomah{% end %} ↔ {% wiki(slug="book-of-revelation") %}Revelation 1+4{% end %}.
Daniel 7 was the only piece of this 4-tradition wire previously cited
only in glossary apparatus; with Daniel translated, all five

surface for the first time.

The white-hair-as-wool iconography of Daniel 7:9 is transferred to

of the most direct verbal-borrowings in the New Testament from the

## The Maccabean dating

Daniel 11:40-45 contains the foundational scholarly argument for the
**167-164 BCE composition window** per Collins 1993. The chapter
accurately describes the Antiochene persecution up through 11:39
(*vaticinium ex eventu*), then **fails to predict the actual end** —
the text predicts Antiochus IV's death in Egypt, but Antiochus
actually died in Persia in 164 BCE during a campaign against the
Parthians. This genuine-prophecy-failure dates the final form to
between the events of 11:39 (167 BCE) and the falsification of 11:40
(164 BCE). The Wheel of Heaven translation preserves the failed
prediction without harmonization.

## Translation choices preserved

- **Bilingual structure preserved** transparently via per-verse
  text_lang ('he' or 'arc')
- **MENE MENE TEKEL UPHARSIN** preserved as Aramaic transliteration
  on surface (uppercase, four words); MT-duplicated reading (not
  OG single)
- **Ancient of Days** preserved as theophanic title (do not
  domesticate to "Eternal One")
- **'one like a son of man'** lowercase + indefinite at Dan 7:13
  (do NOT pre-resolve to NT Christological "Son of Man")
- **'anointed one'** lowercase at Dan 9:25-26 (do NOT pre-resolve to
  "Messiah" / "Christ")
- **MT atnach punctuation** at Dan 9:25 preserved — distinguishing
  the 7-weeks + 62-weeks structure (vs the KJV/NIV '7 + 62 = 69'
  collapse)
- **'abomination of desolation'** preserved as NT-quotation form
  (Mt 24:15 + Mk 13:14)
- **'seventy weeks'** without expansion to "weeks of years" on
  surface (metaphorical-weeks-as-years sense preserved in apparatus)
- **Michael** preserved as proper noun; **'prince'** preserved over
  Greek-translation 'archangel' (which derives from later tradition)
- **BHS verse-numbering** preserved (notably 6:1 = English 5:31)
- ***rabbîm* "many"** ambiguity preserved at Dan 12:2 (not resolved to
  "all" or "some")
- **'time, times, and half a time'** preserved as established English
  idiom (do NOT translate to "3.5 years")
- **1290 days + 1335 days** preserved literally at Dan 12:11-12 (no
  harmonization, no day-year conversion)
- **The unfulfilled prediction at 11:40-45** preserved without
  harmonization — the foundational scholarly evidence for the 167-164
  BCE Maccabean dating per Collins 1993
- NO archaic forms; NO Sitchin/WoH/ancient-astronaut framing on
  surface
- NO Christian-typological pre-resolution of Dan 2:34-35 stone +
  3:25 fourth-man + 7:13 Son of Man + 9:25-26 anointed-one +
  10:4-6 angelic-figure + 11:36-39 + 12:1 antagonist + 12:2
  resurrection
- All Christological + Sitchin + Raëlian-canon reception preserved at
  glossary-apparatus level only (acknowledgment-without-endorsement
  framework)
