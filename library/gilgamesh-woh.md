**Gilgamesh Tablet XI (Wheel of Heaven Translation)** — the
Standard Babylonian recension Flood narrative. The fifth Mesopotamian
text in the WoH corpus (after Enki and Ninmah, the Sumerian Flood
Story, Enki and Ninhursag, and Adapa) and **the single most-canon-
named Mesopotamian text** — the Raëlian canon (per *Yes... I am
Raelian* commentary) names the Epic of Gilgamesh among the texts
"closer to the truth," alongside the Mahābhārata, Kojiki, and Popol
Vuh.

**308 lines** of Standard Babylonian Akkadian per the George 2003
critical edition line-numbering. **Sin-leqi-unninni recension**
(~1200 BCE; underlying Atrahasis-tradition extends back to the
early-2nd-millennium). Primary manuscript witnesses: the **Library
of Ashurbanipal Nineveh tablets** (7th c. BCE; British Museum K series
including K.3375 + K.7752 + K.2252 + Sm. 4302 + others; recovered by
Layard 1849). Rediscovered by **George Smith in 1872** — his 1873
publication *The Chaldean Account of the Deluge* demonstrated the
Mesopotamian antecedent to the biblical Flood narrative.

**Sources:** George 2003 *The Babylonian Gilgamesh Epic* (Oxford, 2
vols) — THE modern critical edition; Thompson 1930 *The Epic of
Gilgamish* (PD critical edition with cuneiform copy + transliteration);
Smith 1873 *The Chaldean Account of the Deluge* (PD discovery
publication); Foster 2005 *Before the Muses* 3rd ed. (scholarly
modern English baseline). Verification pending against George 2003
critical edition + original Nineveh codicology.

**Narrative arc.** Gilgamesh's journey to find Utnapishtim the
immortal flood-survivor; Utnapishtim recounts how the gods (Anu,
Enlil, Ninurta, Ennugi, Ea) decided to flood the earth; Ea's warning
to Utnapishtim through the reed-wall; the boat (*tebû*/*eleppu*)
construction with seven decks; embarkation of family + craftsmen +
"the seed of all living creatures"; the six-day-seven-night flood
from the Anunnaki + Adad's thunder; the landing on Mt Nimush; the
bird-test (dove → swallow → raven); the disembarkation + sacrifice
("the gods gathered like flies"); Enlil's anger + Ea's defense; the
deification + immortality grant to Utnapishtim; Gilgamesh's failure
(the seven-loaves test); the loss of the plant-of-rejuvenation to
the serpent; the return to Uruk.

**Highest cross-corpus density in the Mesopotamian sub-corpus.**
Direct parallels documented per line:

- **Hebrew Bible Genesis 6-9** (Noah narrative) — boat construction,
  animal loading, flood-duration, bird-test, mountain-landing,
  sacrifice, divine-promise. Heidel 1946 *The Gilgamesh Epic and Old
  Testament Parallels* is the foundational comparative study.
- **Sumerian Flood Story** (Ziusudra recension; already in WoH
  Translation as flood-story-woh) — older Mesopotamian flood-tradition.
- **Akkadian Atrahasis** (Lambert-Millard 1969) — Sin-leqi-unninni
  signals at L189 that Utnapishtim and Atra-ḫasīs are the same
  character under alternative standing-epithets ("Surpassingly Wise"
  vs "I-found-life").
- **Berossus *Babyloniaca*** (Hellenistic-period Greek retelling
  preserved in Eusebius via Polyhistor).
- **Qur'an Surah 11:25-49** (Noah narrative; cf. Mt Nimush ↔ al-Jūdī
  Q 11:44).

The **Mt Nimush ↔ Mt Ararat ↔ Mt Ṣapunu ↔ Mt Sinai/Horeb ↔ al-Jūdī**
Northwest-Semitic + Mesopotamian + Qur'anic cosmic-mountain cluster
spans five traditions in five languages.

**The Utnapishtim-deification-vs-Adapa-immortality-loss structural
inversion** is now in production as a bidirectional central entry —
`utnapishtim-deification-vs-adapa-immortality-loss-cross-corpus`.
The same immortality-test pattern, opposite outcomes:

- **Adapa** refuses the divine food Anu offers (per Ea's coaching) →
  humanity loses immortality
- **Utnapishtim** is granted divinity outright at L196 (*Ūta-napišti u
  sinništu-šu lū emû kī ilāni nāši* "Utnapishtim and his wife shall
  become like us — the gods!") → individual won-immortality

Cross-corpus also to Genesis 3 (serpent + tree-of-life-loss); to the
L270 plant-of-rejuvenation + L291 serpent-steals-immortality at the
end of Tablet XI (the serpent etiology paralleling Gen 3).

**Translation choices preserved:**

- **Mt Nimush** (George 2003 reading) on surface; **Mt Niṣir**
  (Thompson 1930, Heidel 1946) documented as overlay textual variant
- **George 2003 line-numbering** convention (vs Thompson/Foster
  narrative-section pagination)
- ***Atra-ḫasīs*** alias preserved at L189 with hyphen + ḫ + ī
- ***ikû*** / ***nindan*** unit-measurements preserved with bracketed
  gloss
- ***abūbu*** "flood" rendered "flood"
- Akkadian divine names preserved: Anu, Enlil, Ea, Adad, Anunnaki,
  Igigi, Ninurta, Ennugi, Atra-ḫasīs, Utnapishtim
- Place names preserved: Shuruppak, Mt Nimush

**Acknowledgment-without-endorsement framework** applied at the two
