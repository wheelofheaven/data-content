**Adapa and the South Wind (Wheel of Heaven Translation)** is the
project's **first Akkadian-source text**. The composition survives in
four fragments spanning roughly a millennium: Fragment A from the
Amarna archive (EA 356, 14th c. BCE, Middle Babylonian); Fragment B
from Nineveh (K.8214+, Neo-Assyrian, the main narrative); and
Fragments C+D from Late Babylonian witnesses. The translation is a
best-effort reconstruction working from Knudtzon 1915 (PD Fragment A),
Picchioni 1981, Izre'el 2001 *LSU* 10, and Foster 2005 *Before the
Muses*; verification against Izre'el's primary critical edition is
pending.

The myth: Adapa, a sage of Eridu and a creature of the god Ea, breaks
the wing of the South Wind by speech-act and is summoned before Anu in
heaven. Ea coaches him to refuse the food and water Anu will offer,
warning they are food and water of *death*. They turn out to be food
and water of *life*. Adapa refuses; humanity loses immortality. The
text pairs with Gen 3:22 (the Yahweh-Elohim withholding the tree of
life from humans after the Eden expulsion) on the inverse-parallel
axis — both narratives explain why humans don't live forever, but they
locate the agency on opposite sides of the divine-human boundary.

**Lines with commentary carry a bracketed marker `[ ]` at the end of
the translated line. Click the marker to open the commentary panel.**
The interlinear view shows the cuneiform alongside the transliteration
when toggled on. Cuneiform is back-rendered from the composite
transliteration via the OGSL mapper — Akkadian source material carries
heavier annotation density (lacunae, parenthesised determinatives,
uncertainty markers) than the Sumerian ETCSL compositions, so a
larger share of lines render with the partial-confidence badge.

79 lines preserved across 4 fragments, with substantial lacunae
between and within the segments. Translation is signed off pending
critical-edition verification — the *Editor's Report* in the source
repository documents the conditions of the reconstruction.

The English translation was drafted by Claude Opus 4.7 and edited
through the Wheel of Heaven Translation Program pipeline (Translator →
Editor → Reviewer). The methodology is documented at
[Source-Text Translation](https://docs.wheelofheaven.world/contributing/content/source-text-translation/).
