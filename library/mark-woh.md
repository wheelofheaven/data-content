**Mark 16 (Wheel of Heaven Translation)** — the Resurrection narrative + the longer-ending textual-critical site. The Raëlian canon engages Mark 16:18 (snake-handling passage) and Mark 16:19 (Ascension) in *Extraterrestrials Took Me To Their Planet* — acknowledgment-without-endorsement framework applied per the established Shi'ur Qomah / Qur'an / Exodus / Matthew precedents.

**The longer-ending textual-critical dispute.** Mark 16 ends at verse 8 (*ephobounto gar* "for they were afraid") in **Codex Vaticanus (B/03)** and **Codex Sinaiticus (א/01)** — the two earliest substantial uncial witnesses. The "longer ending" Mark 16:9-20 (containing the resurrection appearances + the Ascension at v19 + the snake-handling and poison-immunity passage at v18) is ABSENT in Vaticanus + Sinaiticus + Codex Bobiensis (k, Old Latin) + Sinaitic Syriac. The longer ending is PRESENT in Codex Alexandrinus + Codex Bezae + later Byzantine tradition. **Consensus modern critical view** (Metzger UBS Textual Commentary; Aland-Aland; Marcus 2009 AB; Collins 2007 Hermeneia): the longer ending is a 2nd-century scribal addition to harmonize Mark with the other Gospels.

This translation preserves the canonical longer-ending text on the surface (verses 9-20 rendered as canonical Mark 16) — same pattern as Matthew's Conybeare-hypothesis handling at Mt 28:19 — with the textual-criticism dispute documented at the central glossary entry `mark-16-9-20-longer-ending-textual-critical-site` (v2.64.0).

**20 verses** sourced from Vaticanus + Sinaiticus (primary 4th-c. uncial witnesses) + P45 Chester Beatty I (~250 CE, contains substantial Mark) cross-collation + NA28/UBS5/SBLGNT critical apparatus.
