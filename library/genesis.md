**Genesis** (Hebrew: <span class="hebrew">בְּרֵאשִׁית</span>, *Bereshit* —
"In the beginning") is the first book of the Torah and of the Hebrew
Bible. It is the densest single source in the Wheel of Heaven corpus for
the motifs the frame reads as compressed memory of Elohim contact.

## About this edition

- **Translation:** American Standard Version (1901), public domain
- **Source:** Fetched from
  [bible-api.com](https://bible-api.com) via
  `utilities/ingest_bible_book.py` on 2026-04-08
- **Coverage:** 50 chapters, 1,533 verses, fully ingested
- **Primary language:** English (Hebrew original is aspirational;
  will be added as a parallel language in a future pass)
- **Reference format:** `GEN {chapter}:{verse}` — e.g., `GEN 1:26`
  for the famous "Let us make man in our image" verse

The ASV was chosen as a starting translation because it is a
conservative, literal rendering of the Masoretic Hebrew, definitively
public domain (published 1901, predating any copyright term), and
preserves the grammatical distinction between *Elohim* (rendered
"God") and the Tetragrammaton YHWH (rendered "Jehovah" in the ASV).
Other translations such as the Jewish Publication Society 1917 or the
King James Version can be added as additional parallel tracks in
future passes.

## Structure

Genesis is traditionally divided into two main narrative blocks:

- **Primeval history (chapters 1–11)** — creation, the Garden of
  Eden, Cain and Abel, the antediluvian patriarchs, the Nephilim
  (Genesis 6:1–4), the flood, the Table of Nations, and the Tower
  of Babel. This is the section most dense with Wheel of Heaven
  motifs: the plural *Elohim*, creation as making, the Watchers
  narrative seed, the deliberate reset by flood, and the "let us go
  down" at Babel.
- **Patriarchal narratives (chapters 12–50)** — the call of Abraham,
  Isaac, Jacob, and Joseph. Less densely motif-laden from the Wheel
  of Heaven perspective, but foundational for everything the Hebrew
  Bible becomes from Exodus onward.
