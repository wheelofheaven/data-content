**The Song of the Hoe (Wheel of Heaven Translation)** is rendered from the
ETCSL composite transliteration (ETCSL 5.5.4) with the source and the
English translation displayed in parallel.

The hymn opens with **Enlil**, the lord who brings forth what is fitting,
resolving to make the seed of the Land sprout from the ground: he sunders
heaven from earth and earth from heaven, fixes the bond of heaven and
earth (*Dur-an-ki*), and at **Uzu-e-a** — "Where Flesh Came Forth" — sets
the **hoe** (*al*) to the soil, so that the first humans break up through
the ground like plants and are placed in the brick-mould. The remainder is
a tour de force of scribal wordplay that threads the hoe-syllable *al*
through scores of words while praising the hoe as the tool that builds
cities, temples, and the whole order of civilization. The composition is a
Sumerian counterpart to the human-creation accounts of *Enki and Ninmah*
and *Atraḫasīs* (humans made to relieve the gods of toil) and to Genesis
2:7 (the human formed from the ground, *ʾadam* from *ʾadamah*).

**Lines with commentary carry a bracketed marker `[ ]` at the end of the
translated line. Click the marker to open the commentary panel.** Per-line
commentary documents lexical decisions, ETCSL philological options, the
*/al/* paronomasia, and cross-corpus links to the Hebrew Bible, the
Sumerian creation cluster, and *Atraḫasīs*.

The single composition (110 lines, including the sub-line 18A) is signed
