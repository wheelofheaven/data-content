**Ezekiel** opens with one of the most-cited passages in the entire Wheel of Heaven reference corpus — chapter 1's throne-vision, the four *chayyot* (living creatures), the *chashmal* (electrum / amber / gleaming metal), the wheels-within-wheels, and the *kavod* (glory). Chapter 1 will be the project's first non-Genesis WoH Translation, chosen for its strong philological backing (Greenberg's Anchor Bible, Block's NICOT, Zimmerli's Hermeneia), its standing public interest, and its high lens-leverage.

## About this planned translation

- **Status:** Planned. Not yet drafted.
- **Source text:** pointed Hebrew Masoretic text (Westminster Leningrad Codex, Public Domain).
- **Reference format:** `EZK-WOH-{chapter}:{verse}`
- **Roadmap position:** Phase 1 (cross-book pipeline pilot) + Phase 2.

## Scope

Initial scope: chapter 1 (the throne-vision; the pipeline pilot). Then chapter 10 (the throne-vision return; the *kavod* leaves the temple) and chapter 37 (the valley of dry bones — read in *Extra-Terrestrials Took Me To Their Planet* as the biological-creation machine). Extension to other chapters is a reassessment decision.

## How this translation will be produced

This translation will go through the standard Wheel of Heaven Translation
Program pipeline: source acquisition, Translator agent (literal English
applying the production glossary), Editor agent (lexical-decision
adjudication, per-verse commentary, glossary deltas), Reviewer agent
(independent verification with mandatory scholarly citations), and human
sign-off. The full methodology is documented at
[Source-Text Translation](https://docs.wheelofheaven.world/contributing/content/source-text-translation/).

The Wheel of Heaven lens lives in the apparatus — the glossary entries
and per-verse commentary — not in the translated text itself. Accuracy
above lens; where the source admits multiple defensible readings, the
Raëlian canon is the tiebreaker, and every divergence from the standard
translation tradition is recorded with a `claim_type` label and a
philological rationale.
