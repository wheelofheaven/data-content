**Matthew** is the most heavily cited New Testament book in the Raëlian canon. The Parable of the Sower in chapter 13 is the canon's most explicit cosmological reading from the Gospels — read as a description of life being seeded on multiple worlds. The translation will be from the polytonic Greek (Tregelles edition), the oldest Public Domain critical edition of the Greek NT.

## About this planned translation

- **Status:** Planned. Not yet drafted.
- **Source text:** polytonic Greek New Testament (Tregelles 1857–1879, Public Domain).
- **Reference format:** `MAT-WOH-{chapter}:{verse}`
- **Roadmap position:** Phase 4.

## Scope

Initial scope: complete Gospel. Substantial commentary at the messianic passages (chs 1–2, 16–17, 24–26), the Sermon on the Mount (chs 5–7), and the parables of chapter 13.

## How this translation will be produced

This translation will go through the standard Wheel of Heaven Translation
Program pipeline: source acquisition, Translator agent (literal English
applying the production glossary), Editor agent (lexical-decision
adjudication, per-verse commentary, glossary deltas), Reviewer agent
(independent verification with mandatory scholarly citations), and human
sign-off. The full methodology is documented at
[Source-Text Translation](https://docs.wheelofheaven.world/contributing/content/source-text-translation/).

The Wheel of Heaven lens lives in the apparatus — the glossary entries
and per-verse commentary — not in the translated text itself. Accuracy
above lens; where the source admits multiple defensible readings, the
Raëlian canon is the tiebreaker, and every divergence from the standard
translation tradition is recorded with a `claim_type` label and a
philological rationale.
