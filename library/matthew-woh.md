**Matthew (Wheel of Heaven Translation)** — the most heavily cited
New Testament book in the Raëlian canon. *The Role of Christ* engages
Matthew across 17 of its 28 chapters; this translation covers the
eight highest-citation-density chapters as the canon-closure
selection. **The project's first Christian-tradition text and first
Koine Greek source text.**

**The 8 canon-cited chapters:**

- **Mt 1** — the genealogy of Jesus + the virgin-birth narrative
- **Mt 5** — Sermon on the Mount Pt I (Beatitudes; salt and light;
  "not to abolish but to fulfill"; the antitheses)
- **Mt 6** — Sermon on the Mount Pt II (alms; the Lord's Prayer;
  fasting; treasure in heaven; anxiety)
- **Mt 7** — Sermon on the Mount Pt III (judgment; ask-seek-knock;
  the Golden Rule; the narrow gate; the two builders)
- **Mt 13** — **THE EXPLICIT CANON SITE**: the kingdom parables
  (Sower, Tares, Mustard Seed, Leaven, Hidden Treasure, Pearl, Net).
  Raël's *The Role of Christ* reads the Parable of the Sower as
  *"the description of life being seeded on multiple worlds"* — the
  four soil-types interpreted literally as four planet-types
  receiving the Elohim seeding-of-life.
- **Mt 17** — the Transfiguration + the demoniac boy + the
  temple-tax
- **Mt 25** — Ten Virgins + Talents + Sheep and Goats (the final
  judgment)
- **Mt 28** — the Resurrection + the Great Commission

**287 verses across 8 chapters.** The **early-manuscript approach**
parallels the Qur'an translation's Tübingen + Codex
Parisino-petropolitanus method: source the text from the earliest
substantially-complete codices and cross-collate against the earlier
papyri where they preserve target verses.

**Sources:**
- **Codex Vaticanus (B / 03)** — Vatican Library Gr. 1209,
  ~325-350 CE — primary 4th-c. uncial witness; contains complete
  Matthew; the standard NA28/UBS5 critical-text base
- **Codex Sinaiticus (א / 01)** — British Library Add. 43725 + Sinai
  + Leipzig + St. Petersburg, ~330-360 CE — only complete uncial
  codex; secondary primary witness
- **Cross-collation against early papyri** where target verses are
  preserved:
  - **P104** (Oxford P.Oxy 4404; ~150 CE) — among the EARLIEST
    surviving Matthew witnesses, ~150-200 years before the great
    uncials
  - **P64+P67** (Magdalen + Barcelona; late 2nd c. CE) — Mt 5:20-22,
    25-28 + Mt 26
  - **P77** (~200 CE) — Mt 23:30-39
  - **P103** (P.Oxy 4403; late 2nd / early 3rd c. CE) — Mt 13:55-57,
    14:3-5 (cross-collation for our Mt 13)
  - **P45** (Chester Beatty I; ~250 CE) — substantial Matthew
    portions including Mt 20-26 (cross-collation for our Mt 25)

**Critical apparatus reference:** NA28 (Nestle-Aland 28th edition,
2012), UBS5 (United Bible Societies 5th, 2014), SBLGNT (Society of
Biblical Literature, Holmes 2010), THGNT (Tyndale House, Jongkind
2017).

**Three highest-citation-density Raëlian-canon lens-risk sites —
all managed with explicit named verbatim disavowals:**

- **Mt 13:3-23 (the Parable of the Sower)** — **THE single
  most-load-bearing canon-engagement** in the entire Matthew
  translation. Three reading-strata preserved: mainstream
  Christian-tradition (Davies-Allison ICC, Luz Hermeneia, France
  NICNT, Snodgrass 2008); Jewish-historical-critical (Levine 2014
  *Short Stories by Jesus*); Raëlian-canon panspermia. Verbatim
  acknowledgment-without-endorsement per the Shi'ur Qomah *parasangs*
  + Qur'an *inshiqāq al-qamar* + Exodus pillar/parting/manna/theophany
  precedents.
- **Mt 17:1-9 (the Transfiguration)** — Raëlian-canon "Elohim
  manifest in glory + Moses+Elijah appearing" acknowledgment-without-
  endorsement. The *bath-qol* voice at Mt 17:5 is a four-layered
  scriptural allusion: Ps 2:7 (*ben-Yhwh*) + Isa 42:1 (servant-chosen)
  + Deut 18:15 (prophet-like-Moses) + Mt 3:17 (the baptism-voice).
  Allison 1993 *The New Moses*.
- **Mt 28:18-20 (the Great Commission)** — Raëlian-canon engagement +
  the **Conybeare 1901 hypothesis** preserved without adjudication
  (Eusebius's pre-Nicene citations of Mt 28:19 use a shorter
  *en tō onomati mou* form; Conybeare proposed the trinitarian formula
  is a later interpolation. All extant Greek MSS preserve the
  trinitarian formula; the Didache 7 + Justin *1 Apology* 61 attest
  the formula in early-2nd-c. usage). WoH-Translation renders the
  canonical text on surface; documents Conybeare as one scholarly
  position.

**Plus four other major lens-risk sites:**

- **Mt 1:22-23 (the virgin birth + Isa 7:14)** — LXX *parthenos*
  (virgin) vs Hebrew *ʿalmâ* (young woman) philological dispute
  preserved-not-adjudicated. Brown 1993 *The Birth of the Messiah*
  + Levine 2014 *Jewish Annotated New Testament* Jewish-scholarly
  counterpoint.
- **Mt 5:17 ("I came not to abolish but to fulfill")** — locus
  classicus of Jewish-Christian dialogue. Explicit
  Christian-supersessionist disavowal: this verse does NOT support a
  reading where the Torah is superseded. The verb *plērōsai*
  philological breadth preserved (complete / do / fully realize).
- **Mt 6:9-13 (the Lord's Prayer)** — the WoH-Translation renders
  WITHOUT the Byzantine doxology at 6:13b (consensus modern
  critical-text — absent in Vaticanus + Sinaiticus + earliest mss;
  Metzger UBS Textual Commentary). The doxology is documented as a
  textual variant. The *epiousios* hapax at 6:11 — three classical
  readings preserved (daily / for-tomorrow / supersubstantial).
- **Mt 25:40 ("the least of these brothers")** — three reading-strata
  preserved per Gray 1989 *The Least of My Brothers: A History of
  Interpretation*: (A) universalist (all suffering humanity);
  (B) ecclesial-particularist (Christians-in-distress); (C)
  Jewish-particularist (Jewish-people-in-distress). The v45 absence
  of *tōn adelphōn mou* is the key counter-datum.

**Translation choices preserved:**

- *theos* → "God"; *kyrios* → "Lord"; *Iēsous* → "Jesus";
  *Christos* → "Christ" (Greek-Hebrew-Aramaic cognate documented at
  central entries)
- Genealogical text: preserve four women named (Tamar, Rahab, Ruth,
  "wife of Uriah" Bathsheba) per Brown 1993's four readings
- *makarios* in the Beatitudes → "blessed" (KJV/RSV tradition)
- *epiousios* in the Lord's Prayer → "daily" (mainstream English
  tradition); alternatives documented
- Byzantine doxology at Mt 6:13b → OMITTED (critical-text consensus);
  documented as variant
- *raca* at Mt 5:22 → preserved with parenthetical
- *aiōnios* at Mt 25:41, 46 → "eternal" (mainstream); "age-pertaining"
  documented as scholarly alternative
- Trinitarian baptismal formula at Mt 28:19 → preserved as canonical
  text; Conybeare 1901 hypothesis documented at central entry
