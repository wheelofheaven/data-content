**The Qur'an (Wheel of Heaven Translation, early-manuscript selection)**
is the project's **first Islamic-tradition text** and **first
Arabic-source text**. Scope is strictly limited to **Suras 21:1–5,
54:1, and 56:15–24** — the only three Qur'anic passages the Raëlian
canon engages with chapter-and-verse precision (in
*Extra-Terrestrials Took Me To Their Planet*). The project engages
Islam respectfully and technically; this is not a full-Qur'an
translation and does not claim to be.

**The early-manuscript approach.** Rather than working from the modern
Cairo recension (1924 King Fuad edition, based on the late-medieval
*Ḥafṣ ʿan ʿĀṣim* reading-tradition), this translation works from the
**rasm** — the bare consonantal skeleton, no letter-discrimination
dots (*iʿjām*), no vowel marks (*tashkīl*) — of the earliest-surviving
Qur'anic manuscript-witnesses. The dots and vowel marks were added to
Qur'anic manuscripts across the 8th–10th centuries CE; the underlying
rasm in the earliest manuscripts is substantially what was copied in
the 7th century. Sources:

- **Sūrat al-Anbiyāʾ 1–5** ← **Tübingen Fragment Ma VI 165**
  (Universitätsbibliothek Tübingen; Hijazi script;
  radiocarbon-dated **649–675 CE** — within **17–43 years** of the
  traditional Uthmanic codification c. 650 CE; contains Suras
  17–36). Cross-collated against Codex Parisino-petropolitanus.
- **Sūrat al-Qamar 1** + **Sūrat al-Wāqiʿah 15–24** ← **Codex
  Parisino-petropolitanus** (BnF Arabe 328 + St. Petersburg /
  Birmingham / Vatican fragments; Hijazi script; late 7th c.).

Three layers per ayah are preserved in the source files: (1) the
**rasm** (bare consonantal skeleton from the early manuscript); (2)
the modern vocalized Arabic (Hafs ʿan ʿĀṣim reading-tradition, Cairo
1924) supplied as the reference vocalization; (3) **scientific
transliteration** (DMG / ALA-LC convention).

**The textual-criticism context.** The Qur'an's textual transmission
is genuinely scholarly-contested in modern manuscript studies.
Sadeghi-Goudarzi 2010 frames the early manuscripts as evidence of an
early canonical text with limited variation. Hilali 2017 frames the
Sanʿāʾ 1 palimpsest evidence as showing "transmission in flux" in the
first century AH. Earlier scholarship (Wansbrough 1977, Crone-Cook
1977) proposed later-canonization frameworks that recent manuscript
discoveries have substantially nuanced. **This translation preserves
the textual-criticism dispute rather than adjudicating it.**

**The Raëlian-canon citation context.** *Extra-Terrestrials Took Me
To Their Planet* cites Sura 21:1–5 (the eschatological-reckoning
opening), Sura 54:1 (*iqtarabati s-sāʿatu wa-nšaqqa l-qamaru* — "the
Hour has drawn near and the moon has split"), and Sura 56:15–24
(the paradisal-reception passage). The canon's reading of 54:1 as a
literal description of an Elohim-spacecraft propulsion-event that
split the moon is its most-explicit "the Qur'an describes ET tech"
citation. **The WoH-Translation produces the philologically-
disciplined version of these passages, not the canon's pre-filtered
version**, with explicit operational disavowals against
Sitchin/Anunnaki/ET-propulsion readings throughout the glossary
apparatus. The eight canon-citation paragraphs (21:1, 54:1, 56:17,
56:22 and the surrounding passage) all carry explicit
acknowledgment-without-endorsement of the canon's reading — same
pattern as the [Shi'ur Qomah *parasangs*](/library/shiur-qomah-woh/)
precedent.

**Five critical lens-risk sites — all managed with verbatim
disavowals:**

- **54:1 (*inshiqāq al-qamar*, "splitting of the moon")** — three
  reading-strata preserved: mainstream Sunni past-miracle (Bukhārī
  4864, Muslim 2801, Ṭabarī, Ibn Kathīr); modernist eschatological-
  future (al-Manār, Sayyid Quṭb); Raëlian ET-propulsion. Cross-corpus
  Joel 2:30-31 / Mark 13:24-25 / Acts 2:19-20 / Rev 6:12-13. Verbatim
  Sitchin / NASA-apologetic / Christian-supersessionist disavowals.
- **56:22 (*ḥūr ʿīn*, the "wide-eyed companions")** — five
  reading-strata: classical tafsīr / modern Asad-Pickthall-Arberry /
  **Luxenberg 2000 "white grapes" academically rejected** (Stewart
  2008, Neuwirth 2003, Saleh 2008) / **popular Western "72 virgins"
  stereotype disavowed** (the number 72 is from later weak ḥadīth
  Tirmidhī 2562, NOT in the Qur'an) / Raëlian disavowed.
- **56:17 (*wildān muḫalladūn*, "youths eternal")** — Luxenberg
  "fruits-everlasting" + Raëlian "cloning-immortality" disavowed.
- **21:1 (*iqtaraba li-n-nāsi ḥisābuhum*, "the reckoning has drawn
  near for humanity")** — Christian-supersessionist + Raëlian
  Age-of-Apocalypse disavowals. Cross-corpus Mark 13:32-33 / Matt
  24:36-42 / 1 Thess 5:1-3 / 2 Pet 3:3-10 / Joel 2:1-2.
- **The Allah entry** — genuine Semitic-cognate documented (Arabic
  *Allāh* < *al-ilāh*; Hebrew *Elohim*; Aramaic *Elāh*; Syriac
  *Alāhā* — all from shared *√ʾ-l-h*). Used by Arabic-speaking
  Christians, Jews, and Muslims. Disavow simplistic-identification
  (Allah = YHWH; Allah = Trinitarian Father) — the Qur'anic Allah
  has its own theological profile (strict *tawḥīd*; rejection of
  trinitarian and incarnational claims).

The translation is **best-effort reconstruction** from published
facsimiles + the Corpus Coranicum digital apparatus + Sadeghi-Bergmann
2010 + Sadeghi-Goudarzi 2010 + Hilali 2017 + Déroche 2009/2014 +
Neuwirth 2014 + Reynolds 2008/2011. Verification against the original
manuscript codicology (Tübingen UB; BnF Paris; National Library of
Russia St. Petersburg; Cadbury Research Library Birmingham; BAV
Vatican) is pending; corrections welcome.
