**Shi'ur Qomah** ("Measurement of the Body") is the Hebrew esoteric corpus from the Hekhalot literature that the Raëlian canon implicitly engages when it cites "the Kabbalah" for the *parasangs*-measurement and the "height of the creator" passages (*The End of the World*, chapter *At the Root of All Religions*). This translation closes one of the genuine source-tensions named in the *Reference corpus of the Raëlian canon* wiki entry: the canon generically names "the Kabbalah" where the actual textual source is the Shi'ur Qomah body of literature.

## About this planned translation

- **Status:** Planned. Not yet drafted.
- **Source text:** Hebrew (specific source edition TBD at translation start).
- **Reference format:** `SHQM-WOH-{section}:{line}`
- **Roadmap position:** Phase 5.

## Scope

Initial scope: a curated selection of the *parasangs* passages and related cosmological material the Raëlian canon implicitly engages. Specific source edition and section selection to be determined at translation start. This is **not** a complete book translation — the Shi'ur Qomah/Hekhalot corpus is large, fragmentary, and would require dedicated scholarly competence the project has not yet committed to building.

## How this translation will be produced

This translation will go through the standard Wheel of Heaven Translation
Program pipeline: source acquisition, Translator agent (literal English
applying the production glossary), Editor agent (lexical-decision
adjudication, per-verse commentary, glossary deltas), Reviewer agent
(independent verification with mandatory scholarly citations), and human
sign-off. The full methodology is documented at
[Source-Text Translation](https://docs.wheelofheaven.world/contributing/content/source-text-translation/).

The Wheel of Heaven lens lives in the apparatus — the glossary entries
and per-verse commentary — not in the translated text itself. Accuracy
above lens; where the source admits multiple defensible readings, the
Raëlian canon is the tiebreaker, and every divergence from the standard
translation tradition is recorded with a `claim_type` label and a
philological rationale.
