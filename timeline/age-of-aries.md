## I. The Age Itself

The tenth age is the age of the ram and of those who speak for the Elohim.

The Age of Aries runs from –2,370 to –210, a span of 2,160 years, following immediately upon the Age of Taurus. It is, by a substantial margin, the longest-sustained narrative arc in the Hebrew Bible: most of what the conventional tradition calls "biblical history" — the Exodus, the wandering, the conquest of Canaan, the period of the Judges, the united monarchy under Saul and David and Solomon, the divided kingdoms of Israel and Judah, the great prophetic tradition, the Babylonian exile, and the Second Temple period — unfolds within its boundaries. It is also the age during which, elsewhere in the world, the civilizations that had consolidated during Taurus reach their classical expressions: the Egyptian New Kingdom, the Mesopotamian empires, the Persian imperial system, the flowering of Chinese dynastic civilization under the Zhou, the Indian Vedic period and the emergence of the Upanishads, the Olmec and early Maya in Mesoamerica, the Greek classical period at its very end. Aries is the age in which the post-flood civilizational project enters its mature phase across every lineage.

The age is named for its constellation: *Aries*, the Ram. From approximately –2,000 to roughly the beginning of the common era, observers looking eastward at the sunrise on the spring equinox would have seen the sun emerge against the stars of the Ram. The bull-cults of the Taurean period gave way to ram-cults and ram-symbolism. The Egyptian Khnum, the ram-headed creator god. The Israelite Pesach lamb. The Greek golden fleece. The Celtic ram-headed gods. The ram caught in the thicket that substituted for Isaac at the sacrifice, that most iconic moment of the Taurus-Aries transition itself, when the animal of the coming age arrived at precisely the right moment to preserve the lineage of the previous age's tested patriarch.

This chapter will walk the Age of Aries in several distinct movements. The first follows the Eden lineage: the migration to Egypt and the Exodus under Moses, the giving of the Law at Sinai, the conquest of Canaan, the Judges, the monarchy, and the prophetic tradition. The second addresses the Hebrew people's failure to fulfill the mission they had been given. The third treats Persia and Greece as Council-cultivated counterweights. The fourth treats the other world regions where Elohim presence continued. The chapter closes with the preparation for the Piscean age.

## II. The Semi-Primitive Inheritance

The Eden lineage entered the Age of Aries in a substantially reduced condition. The Taurus chapter described the two successive catastrophes: the Tower of Babel scattering, and the Sodom and Gomorrah strike. Abraham had emerged as the tested and reliable leadership. But Abraham was a herdsman. The civilization his forebears had built existed now only in fragmentary cultural memory.

The opening centuries of Aries were a period of slow consolidation. Abraham's son Isaac, his grandson Jacob (renamed Israel), and the twelve sons of Jacob who would become the progenitors of the twelve tribes populate the patriarchal narratives of Genesis 23 through 50. The transition to the Exodus situation came through Joseph, whose descent into Egypt and eventual rise to high administrative position relocated the entire Eden lineage into Egypt, where they would multiply substantially while being reduced progressively to a servile condition.

The source describes the end state: *"The people chosen as the most intelligent had lost their most brilliant minds and had become slaves to neighboring tribes who were more numerous since they had not undergone the same destruction. It was thus necessary to restore dignity to the people of Israel by returning their land to them."* The alliance faced a choice: let the lineage disappear through absorption, or intervene to restore its independence. The Exodus is the record of the choice the alliance made.

## III. The Burning Bush

The Exodus narrative opens with Moses. The decisive moment occurs when Moses, having fled Egypt and having spent years in Midian as a shepherd, encounters what the text calls the burning bush. The Raëlian source reads this directly: *"A rocket landed in front of him, and his description corresponds to what a Brazilian tribesman might say today if we were to land before him in a flying vessel illuminating the trees without burning them."*

The reading is functional. The Elohim alliance has dispatched a small craft to the Sinai desert. The craft descends, generates illumination that the text describes as flame, and remains without igniting the surrounding vegetation. The "angel of Yahweh" who speaks to Moses from within the craft is the alliance officer assigned to the initial contact. The voice that identifies itself as "the Elohim of Abraham, the Elohim of Isaac, and the Elohim of Jacob" is the alliance officer identifying the political continuity of the mission. And the operational instructions that follow — return to Egypt, confront Pharaoh, demand the release, lead them out — are the mission briefing.

From this moment forward, Moses is the human operational partner of the alliance for the duration of the Exodus and the wilderness period. He is not a mystic receiving private revelations. He is a field commander receiving specific operational instructions from advanced personnel who have the technology to continue supporting the operation in real time.

## IV. The Plagues and the Departure

The plagues of Egypt are, on the source's reading, direct technical interventions by the alliance to force Pharaoh's capitulation. What the Hebrew text describes in the vocabulary of its period as divine acts of judgment, the corpus reads as specific technological operations conducted by an advanced civilization.

The plagues follow a pattern of escalating severity. The final plague involves the selective death of specific individuals across the Egyptian population. The Israelites were protected through the ritual of the Passover lamb, whose blood on the doorposts identified Hebrew households to be spared. The Passover ritual itself is, on the corpus's reading, the operational identification protocol by which the alliance's targeting systems distinguished protected households from target households. The Passover is preserved in Jewish tradition as the annual commemoration of this moment — one of the clearest examples of how an operational procedure at a moment of direct alliance intervention can be encoded in religious practice and maintained for three thousand years.

The Israelites departed guided by two specific phenomena: *"And Yahweh went before them by day in a pillar of a cloud, to lead them the way; and by night in a pillar of fire"* (Exodus 13:21). The source reads this directly: a single Elohim craft accompanying the departing column, providing navigation by day (visible as a cloud-like formation) and illumination by night (visible as a luminous column).

The crossing of the *Yam Suf* used the same technology. *"The smoke emitted behind the people of Israel made a curtain, which slowed down their pursuers."* The pillar repositioned itself between the Israelites and the Egyptians. *"Then the crossing of the water was made possible by a repulsion beam, which cleared a passageway."* The Israelites crossed. The Egyptians followed. The beam was turned off, and the pursuing army was drowned.

## V. The Wilderness and the Provisions

The Israelites' subsequent years in the wilderness were sustained by continuing alliance support.

The food was manna. The source reads this directly: *"The manna was nothing more than pulverized synthetic chemical food, which when spread on the ground, swelled with the early morning dew."* The alliance was distributing a synthetic food concentrate, delivered from above during the night.

Water came through specific interventions. The source reads Moses's staff as an instrument: *"The staff which allowed Moses to draw water from the rock... was nothing but a detector of underground water pools similar to those which you use at present to find oil."*

The brass serpent that Moses sets up as a treatment for snakebites receives a similar reading: *"As soon as someone was bitten, he 'looked' at the 'serpent of brass', that is to say, a syringe was brought to him, and he was injected with serum."*

These details establish that the wilderness period was a time of sustained alliance support for a dependent population. The alliance provided food, water, medical care, and navigation — and the stability required for the establishment of the religious and legal framework that would sustain the lineage through the subsequent centuries.

## VI. Sinai and the Law

The central event of the wilderness period is the giving of the Law at Mount Sinai.

*"And mount Sinai was altogether on a smoke, because Yahweh descended upon it in fire: and the smoke thereof ascended as the smoke of a furnace, and the whole mount quaked greatly"* (Exodus 19:18). The source reads this as a formal alliance audience. The atmospheric and visual effects are produced by a substantial craft descending. The strict prohibitions against approaching the mountain reflect security precautions. The source adds: *"The creators were afraid of being invaded or maltreated by human beings. It was therefore essential that they be respected, even venerated, so that they would be in no danger."*

Moses alone was permitted to approach. The alliance officers wore pressurized suits because the terrestrial atmosphere was not suitable for their physiology. The content of the meetings was the Law — the Ten Commandments and the extensive legal code. The source notes: *"Because the Israelites were so primitive, they needed laws regarding morals and especially hygiene."* The Law was functional guidance packaged as religious commandment, because religious commandment was the form in which it could most reliably be preserved.

## VII. The Horns of Moses

When Moses came down from Sinai, Exodus 34:29 records that his face had been changed. The Hebrew phrase *ki karan or panav* contains the verb *karan*, formed from the noun *keren*, which means *horn*. The phrase can be read as "the skin of his face horned" or as "the skin of his face shone with rays." Both readings are grammatically legitimate.

The Septuagint rendered the phrase as "shone." Jerome's Vulgate rendered it as *cornuta* — "horned." For a thousand years, Moses was depicted in Western art with horns, culminating in Michelangelo's famous statue in San Pietro in Vincoli.

The Wheel of Heaven reading approaches the question from a different angle. Moses descended from Sinai at the start of the Age of Aries. The animal of Aries is the ram. The symbolic marker of the age is the ram's horn. Moses is the horn-bearing figure of the new age — the prophet of the Ram Age. The "rays of light" reading captures the transfiguration through contact with the alliance. The "horns" reading captures the precessional symbolism. The Hebrew text preserves both meanings at once.

The shofar, the ram's-horn trumpet used in Jewish ritual, is the surviving liturgical expression of the same symbolism. Moses descends from Sinai with the Law in his hand and the horn of the ram on his face. The shofar is the same symbol, preserved in liturgical practice, continuing to mark the Age of Aries for as long as the age endured.

## VIII. The Tabernacle and the Ark

The Law included specific construction specifications for the Tabernacle and the Ark of the Covenant.

The source reads the Tabernacle as *"a meeting tent where people brought food and gifts as a pledge of submission."* The alliance officers used it as their working base on the ground. The food and gifts were partly symbolic and partly literal provisioning: *"This team of creators was going to live on the Earth for some time, and they wished to eat fresh food... I suppose you might call it colonization."*

The Ark of the Covenant had specific electromagnetic properties. Men who touched it without authorization died. It emitted visible phenomena. It generated an audible voice from between the cherubim. It could be used in warfare. It stopped the Jordan's flow. It brought down the walls of Jericho. The corpus reads these descriptions together: the Ark was a technological artifact housing a power source, communications equipment, and directed-energy capabilities, provided with strict handling protocols because improper handling would be dangerous.

## IX. The Conquest and Its Weapons

The Israelites crossed the Jordan using the same water-repulsion technology used at the Red Sea. The fall of Jericho was an ultrasonic resonance attack: *"By using highly amplified ultrasonic waves, one can knock down any concrete wall. This is what was done using a very complicated instrument, which the Bible calls a 'trumpet.'"*

The Battle of Beth-Horon included aerial bombing: *"Yahweh cast down great stones from heaven upon them"* (Joshua 10:11). The source notes: *"This full scale bombing, as indicated, killed more people than the swords of the Israelites."* The reported suspension of the sun's motion is read as an observational artifact of a very rapid campaign: *"This simply means that it was a flash war, which lasted only one day."*

## X. Samson and the Later Judges

The source reads Samson as an experimental alliance project in direct genetic modification: *"A young man was chosen at his birth to live in a specific way, and to take a particular nourishment, so that after having studied his brain thoroughly, the creators could give him telepathic powers from their vessel."* Samson's strength was a real capability whose source was a telepathic link between Samson and alliance operators who could enhance his physical capabilities through direct neural stimulation. The cutting of his hair by Delilah was the external signal by which the connection was severed.

## XI. The Kingdom and the Prophets

The transition to the monarchy under Samuel, Saul, David, and Solomon, and the subsequent decline through the divided kingdoms and the Babylonian exile, produced the prophetic tradition as the distinctive religious form of the later Aries period.

Elijah was taken up by an alliance craft (2 Kings 2:11). Ezekiel's vision (Ezekiel 1) is read by the source as the clearest prophetic account of an Elohim craft — *"The Flying Saucers of Ezekiel."* Isaiah and Jeremiah are similarly treated as prophets in the alliance-contact tradition. The Isaiah passages concerning the slaying of Leviathan (Isaiah 27:1), discussed in the Gemini chapter, are part of this corpus. The passages concerning the eventual return of the Jewish people from exile are read as alliance-provided information about the long-term restoration.

## XII. The Unfulfilled Commission

At this point a structural pause is necessary, because the Aries narrative cannot be understood in its later movements without recognizing the failure that reshaped the age's second half.

The Eden lineage had been given a message. The message was not theirs alone. It was, in the source's framing, *"a message destined for all humanity."* The Scriptures that Moses and his successors had received were humanity's inheritance held in trust.

The source's assessment is direct: *"People of Israel, we removed you from the clutches of the Egyptians, and you did not show yourselves worthy of our confidence; we entrusted you with a message destined for all humanity, and you jealously kept it instead of spreading it abroad."*

The Eden lineage developed a religious tradition whose characteristic features included a strong sense of chosenness, elaborate ritual distinctions separating the people from their neighbors, and a particular resistance to the incorporation of non-Israelites into the covenant community. These features, appropriate to the specific historical moment in which they developed, became the basis of an increasingly exclusive self-understanding that treated the alliance's gifts as the lineage's private possession rather than as a trust held on behalf of humanity as a whole.

The consequences of this failure shape every subsequent age. The Council cultivated rival civilizations as instruments of pressure. The alliance developed a pluriform prophetic strategy deploying multiple prophetic figures across multiple cultures. The Age of Pisces would inaugurate the work of spreading the message to the gentile world through explicitly missionary religions.

## XIII. Persia and Greece: The Cultivated Rivals

The Council's response to the Hebrew failure was specific. The source states: *"If the Hebrew people were dominated by the Persians and the Greeks, it was because of their lack of faith. Consequently, the Elohim punished the Hebrews by sending some of their 'angels' amongst the Persians and Greeks to help those nations to progress technologically. This explains the great moments in the history of those two civilizations. The archangel Michael was the leader of the delegation, which was helping the Persians: 'Michael, one of the chief princes, came to help me; and I remained there with the kings of Persia.' Daniel 10:13."*

The reading reverses the conventional interpretation of Daniel 10:13. In the Raëlian reading, Michael leads a delegation actively supporting the Persian kingdom, and the text's statement that the messenger "remained there with the kings of Persia" is taken at face value. Daniel 10:20 names the Greek follow-on explicitly: *"Now will I return to fight with the prince of Persia: and when I am gone forth, lo, the prince of Grecia shall come."*

The Persian Empire under Cyrus, Darius, and their successors became the largest and most sophisticated political organization humanity had yet produced. The Persian conquest of Babylon in 539 BCE ended the Jewish exile — Cyrus's decree permitting the return is, on the Raëlian reading, a direct expression of the alliance's ongoing concern for the Hebrews even as the Council used Persian power against them.

Zoroastrianism is the Persian religious legacy of the Elohim delegation that Michael led. Zoroaster received what the tradition describes as direct revelation from Ahura Mazda. The Raëlian framework reads Ahura Mazda as the Council's representative to the Persian civilization. The specific theological features of Zoroastrianism — cosmic dualism, final judgment, resurrection, angelology — shaped Jewish theology during the exile in ways visible across the later prophetic and apocalyptic literature.

Greece follows Persia in the biblical narrative. Greek mythology is identified by the source as containing "important testimonies" — the Olympian pantheon read as memory of alliance officers operating from a Greek base. Greek philosophy — the pre-Socratics, Socrates, Plato, Aristotle — is the intellectual fruit of sustained Elohim cultivation of the Greek lineage. The Hellenistic synthesis following Alexander's conquests created the cultural matrix within which the Piscean-age transition would take place.

Alexander the Great, on his coinage, was depicted with ram's horns — another instance of the Aries-age symbolism attaching to a consequential political figure.

## XIV. The Other Regions: Himalayas, Andes, East Asia

The source is explicit: *"This is especially true in those countries where the creators had bases — in the Andes, in the Himalayas, in Greece where Greek mythology also contains important testimonies, as well as in the Buddhist and Islamic religions and among the Mormons."*

The Himalayan base is connected to the chain of sacred mountain traditions — Mount Kailash as the abode of Shiva, the Tibetan *beyul*, the Chinese Buddhist and Taoist mountain traditions. The spiritual traditions of the Indian subcontinent that reached literary expression during Aries (the Upanishads, the Buddhist canon, the early Jain tradition) carry features the corpus reads as the legacy of alliance contact: contemplative practice, ethical frameworks rooted in specific techniques, institutional structures (monasticism, guru-disciple transmission) capable of preserving the teaching across generations.

Buddhism, founded by Siddhartha Gautama in the sixth or fifth century BCE, is explicitly identified by the source as testifying to the alliance's work. Hindu tradition includes figures whose resemblance to Elohim officers is substantial — Indra, Agni, Varuna — and the later tradition of the *avatars* preserves the pattern of alliance intervention at critical moments.

The Andean base is connected to the Chavín culture, the Nazca geoglyphs (read as landing markers for alliance aircraft), and the elaborate astronomical knowledge preserved into the Inca period. East Asian civilizations produced the Taoist tradition of "immortals" inhabiting mountain bases — a pattern consistent with Elohim presence in specific locations. The Korean Dangun myth and Japanese Shinto preserve regional expressions of the same memory.

## XV. The End of Aries and the Preparation for Pisces

The later centuries of Aries saw the conditions for the Piscean age being laid down across multiple civilizations simultaneously.

In the Jewish tradition, the exile and the Second Temple period produced the synagogue, Torah study, the messianic expectation, and the apocalyptic literature. In Greece, the classical philosophical tradition matured and spread. In India, Buddhism became a major civilizational force. In China, the Han dynasty unified and preserved the classical texts.

The convergence of these developments across different civilizations is what Karl Jaspers identified as the "Axial Age." The corpus reads this convergence as the alliance's preparation for the Piscean-age intervention. The ground was being prepared. The religious vocabularies were being developed. The Hebrews' failure to spread their message meant that the Piscean-age intervention could not rely on the Eden lineage alone. Multiple prophetic figures, across multiple cultures, would be needed.

The Aries age closes with the Jewish people under Roman occupation, the Second Temple standing but compromised, the messianic expectation at fever pitch, Greek culture saturating the eastern Mediterranean, and the Zoroastrian-inflected theological framework providing categories for apocalyptic expectation. The ram was yielding to the fish.

## XVI. The Text and Its Signals

Several features of the texts deserve remark beyond those already treated.

The proliferation of the word *malakh* — "messenger" or "angel" — across the Aries-period narrative reflects the alliance's sustained operational contact with the lineage. The word's etymology is the same as "messenger" in ordinary Hebrew usage. The theological freight it would later acquire is a post-biblical development.

The increasing tendency to refer to "Yahweh" through intermediaries rather than as present in person reflects the changing operational relationship. The direct contact of the wilderness period gave way to a more distant coordinating role, maintained through the prophetic tradition.

The Ark of the Covenant disappears at the Babylonian destruction of the Temple. The corpus reads this as a deliberate alliance action — the hardware was not to fall into Babylonian hands.

The Book of Tobit presents the figure of Raphael, whom the source identifies specifically: *"In the Book of Tobit in the Apocrypha, one of the creators' robots named Raphael also came to test humanity's reaction towards its creators."* Raphael was an artificial intelligence — a robot — dispatched by the alliance to interact with the protagonist. The detail that Raphael "did neither eat nor drink" reflects his non-biological nature.

The source identifies the Kabbalah as *"the closest book to the truth"* — a striking claim that establishes, as a matter of framework, that the Jewish mystical tradition preserves substantially accurate alliance-transmitted content in its vocabulary of *sephirot*, its treatment of the divine name, and its teachings about cosmic emanation.

## XVII. What Aries Is

Aries is the age of the prophet. It is the age in which the alliance's relationship with the Eden lineage matures from direct operational partnership into the sustained prophetic tradition that would carry the relationship forward across two thousand years. Moses at the burning bush and at Sinai is the template for every subsequent prophetic encounter.

Aries is the age of the Law. The legal and ethical framework from Sinai is the alliance's most substantial intervention in the cultural development of the Eden lineage. That the Law has worked — that the Jewish people have survived and flourished across three thousand years — is evidence of the care with which the alliance designed its provisions.

Aries is the age of the ram. Moses descends from Sinai with horns. The shofar becomes the central liturgical instrument. The Passover lamb, the substitutionary ram at the Sacrifice of Isaac, the ram-headed Khnum, the Greek golden fleece, Alexander's ram horns — all reflect the same astronomical fact.

Aries is the age of the failed mission. The Hebrews kept the message jealously rather than spreading it abroad. This failure necessitated the Council's cultivation of Persia and Greece, and the alliance's pluriform Piscean-age strategy.

Aries is the age of the world religions' foundations. Zoroastrianism, Buddhism, early Hinduism, Greek philosophy, Chinese religious thought, the Kabbalah — all have Aries-age origins. The Axial Age convergence is the effect of coordinated alliance preparation for the Piscean intervention.

The next age is the age in which the Piscean prophets arrive. Jesus of Nazareth, on the Raëlian reading, is an alliance project of specific character. His fishermen disciples — the Greek word for "fish," *ichthys*, becoming the early Christian symbol — carried the mission forward into the world the Aries age had prepared. That age is the Age of Pisces, and it is the subject of the chapter that follows.
