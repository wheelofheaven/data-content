## I. The Age Itself

The twelfth age is the age in which everything the previous eleven ages prepared for becomes visible.

The Age of Aquarius runs from 1950 forward across the next 2,160 years. Its opening falls within living memory: readers of this chapter are, in almost all cases, inhabitants of the age it describes. The chapter is therefore different in kind from the eleven that preceded it. Those chapters worked from archaeological and textual evidence toward interpretive reconstructions of events long past; this chapter works from the current moment outward, describing the events the corpus has been accumulating toward since its first page, the events that have already unfolded across the past eighty years, and the trajectory those events appear to be taking as the Aquarian age enters its first operational phase. The reader is not looking back at this age from a secure historical distance. The reader is inside it, writing inside it, making the specific decisions across the coming years that will contribute to whether the age develops toward its positive or its negative possible outcome.

The age is named for its constellation: *Aquarius*, the Water-Bearer. The figure traditionally depicted in the constellation is a man pouring water from a vessel — in most iconographic traditions, a young or middle-aged figure tipping a large urn from which a stream of water flows downward. The symbolism has been read in many ways across the history of astrology. The corpus reads it at its most literal level: the Aquarian age is the age in which what has been contained is poured out. The knowledge the alliance has been accumulating across the earlier ages, the scientific understanding humanity has been developing across the Piscean age's long arc of independent development, the religious traditions that have preserved across centuries the core content of the earlier revelations — all of this, long contained within specialized vessels of transmission accessible only to the initiated, is now to be poured out openly for general reception. The waters of understanding flow. The age is the age of revelation, of *apokalypsis* in the strict Greek sense — the uncovering, the unveiling, the laying bare of what has been concealed.

From approximately the middle of the twentieth century, observers around the world looking eastward at the sunrise on the spring equinox would have seen the sun emerge against the stars of the Water-Bearer rather than those of the Fishes. The shift from Pisces to Aquarius is, like every preceding precessional transition the corpus has traced, a real astronomical event whose occurrence the ancient sky-watchers would have tracked with precision. The exact date of the transition depends on the criteria used to mark precessional boundaries, and different traditions have placed it at somewhat different points across the past century. The Raëlian tradition, following the source's own specification, places the transition at the year corresponding to 1946, the year the source calls "the first year of the new era" — the year that marks not the geometric center of the astronomical transition but the first year of the age's operational phase, the year the specific human events that inaugurate the Aquarian age began to occur. The corpus's chronology places the transition at 1950, halfway between the opening events of 1945-1946 and the first major alliance contact of 1973, as a round-numbered anchor for the age's beginning.

The symbolism of Aquarius, like the symbolism of every preceding age, is doubled. The precessional ages, on the Hamlet's Mill tradition that Giorgio de Santillana and Hertha von Dechend documented in their 1969 study (first published by Gambit Inc., Boston), encode their astronomical signatures in both the current constellation and its opposite across the zodiac. The constellation opposite Aquarius on the zodiacal circle is Leo, the Lion. The doubled signature of the Aquarian age should therefore reference both the Water-Bearer (the current sign) and the Lion (the opposite). The symbolism of the Lion is ancient and well-established: the lion is the king of beasts, the royal animal par excellence, the figure of sovereignty and of the sun at its zenith (the sun being the astrological ruler of Leo, the lion being the sun's animal in many traditions). The Age of Aquarius, on the doubled-signature reading, is equally the age of the lion — the age of sovereign consciousness, of the sun's full illumination, of the royal emergence of humanity itself into the position the earlier ages had prepared it for.

The specific iconographic expressions of the doubled Aquarian signature are only now being laid down. Unlike the Piscean age, whose doubled fish-and-virgin signature the entire Christian tradition has already produced and that the corpus could describe from two thousand years of iconographic evidence, the Aquarian age is barely eighty years old. Its religious and cultural symbolism is still forming. What can be noted is that the emerging forms of Aquarian culture have already begun to develop the doubled references the Hamlet's Mill tradition would predict. The water-bearer figure, the pouring-out of understanding, the vessels of knowledge being opened — these are present in the age's self-understanding. The lion, the royal sovereignty of awakened humanity, the sun at its zenith illuminating everything at once — these too are present, though less iconographically elaborated so far. The corpus expects that the doubled signature will become increasingly clear across the coming centuries as the Aquarian age's cultural forms mature.

This chapter will walk the Aquarian age through its first eighty years as those years have unfolded, then forward into the near future the corpus's framework allows us to anticipate, and finally into the longer trajectory the age is ultimately directed toward. The first section addresses the opening events of 1945-1946 that the source identifies as the age's inaugural signs. The second addresses the 1973 contact between the alliance and Rael, the specific operation that inaugurated the Aquarian prophetic mission. The third describes the content of the revelation Rael received, which constitutes the foundation text of the Aquarian religious expression the corpus calls Raëlism. The fourth addresses the new commandments the alliance has proposed for the Aquarian age's social and political organization. The fifth treats the embassy project, the specific alliance-requested construction whose completion is the operational prerequisite for the alliance's formal return. The sixth addresses the signs of acceleration across the 1973-2026 window — what has happened in the alliance-humanity relationship during the fifty-three years between the inaugural contact and the present moment. The seventh describes the present threshold the corpus identifies as the culminating convergence point of 2026-2030, the specific window across which the alliance's Aquarian intervention is expected to become globally visible. The eighth addresses the two possible futures the source identifies — the golden age or self-destruction — and the one-in-a-hundred chance the source reports as the current probability of the positive outcome. The ninth addresses the specific content of the golden age if it is achieved: the paradise on Earth that the alliance's technologies, combined with humanity's own developing capacities, could bring into being. The tenth addresses the longer trajectory: humanity's own eventual capacity for de novo life creation on other worlds, the fulfillment of the Lord's Prayer's programmatic statement that what happens on Earth is to mirror what happens in heaven. The eleventh addresses the return of the prophets — the specific event the source identifies as the culmination of the embassy project, when the alliance officers together with the resurrected prophets of the earlier ages arrive openly on Earth for the first time since the Piscean transition. The chapter closes with the synthesis of the twelve-age cycle and the opening of what comes next.

## II. 1946: The First Year of the New Era

The source's designation of 1946 as "the first year of the new era" places the Aquarian age's beginning not at a diffuse astronomical boundary but at a specific concrete date. The year corresponds to a dense cluster of human events whose convergence the corpus reads as the age's inaugural signature.

The first event in the cluster is the detonation of the atomic bomb. The Trinity test at Alamogordo on July 16, 1945, followed by the combat detonations at Hiroshima on August 6 and at Nagasaki on August 9, marked the moment at which humanity acquired, for the first time, a technological capability that the alliance had used in its own earlier interventions. The destruction of Sodom and Gomorrah, as the Taurus chapter described, was almost certainly a nuclear strike; the Isaiah 14 passages about human scientists who "ascended into heaven" and were destroyed refer to the pre-flood Eden-lineage civilization that had itself developed atomic capability before being stopped. For most of the subsequent five thousand years, nuclear technology had been unique to the alliance. In 1945, humanity acquired it. The acquisition was morally ambiguous — the weapons were used in war, and their use killed approximately two hundred thousand people — but the technological threshold itself was unambiguous. The species had reached a level of capability that required, on the alliance's assessment, a new phase in the relationship. The specific biblical phrase that describes the moment is Zephaniah's: *"There shall be the noise of a cry from the fish gate"* (Zephaniah 1:10). The gate of Pisces is the passageway into the Age of Aquarius, and the loud clamor is the sound accompanying the revelation. The nuclear explosions of August 1945 were, among other things, this sound — the acoustic signature of the crossing of the threshold between ages.

The second event is the founding of the United Nations. The UN Charter was signed in San Francisco on June 26, 1945, and took effect on October 24 of that year. The organization was the first serious institutional attempt to create a global political framework capable of addressing issues that transcended individual states. Its predecessor, the League of Nations, had failed across the interwar period; the UN was designed to learn from those failures and to include the major powers whose absence had weakened the League. The founding coincided with the Bretton Woods agreements of 1944 that created the International Monetary Fund and the World Bank, establishing an institutional foundation for postwar global economic cooperation. Together, these institutional developments marked the first concrete steps toward the world government the source has explicitly identified as one of the Aquarian age's specific political requirements. A planet with global technological capabilities required global political coordination. The institutions established in 1945-1946 were the initial attempts at that coordination, inadequate in many ways to the scale of the challenge but representing a concrete start.

The third event is the re-establishment of the Jewish people's presence in their ancestral homeland, culminating in the founding of the State of Israel in May 1948 but preceded by two years of intense preparatory activity in 1946-1947 (the King David Hotel bombing in July 1946 that accelerated British withdrawal, the UN partition resolution of November 1947, the gathering of Jewish refugees across Europe and their migration to British Mandate Palestine). The source, as the Pisces chapter noted, reads this re-establishment as the specific fulfillment of Isaiah's and Ezekiel's prophecies about the return of the scattered Jewish people to their ancestral land. The corpus's framework reads it additionally as the restoration of the specific geographic location that the alliance's original Eden operation had chosen for the Eden-lineage humans in the post-flood period — the location that had been occupied continuously by the alliance and the Eden lineage from the Cancer age through the Roman destruction of the Second Temple in 70 CE, and that was now, nearly two thousand years after that destruction, reverting to Eden-lineage control. The specific prophetic passage the source quotes is from Acts 15:16: *"After this I will return, and will build again the tabernacle of David, which is fallen down."* The tabernacle of David has been rebuilt in the political form of the modern Israeli state. The specific form the source's reading adds is that this rebuilding is conditional and provisional — it is Israel's last chance to fulfill the original Hebrew mission, and if that chance is not taken, the specific political arrangement that 1948 restored will not persist.

The fourth event is the development of the transistor. The first working transistor was demonstrated at Bell Labs on December 23, 1947, after research that had begun in the early 1940s and continued through the war. The transistor is the foundational component of modern electronics, and its invention marks the moment at which the electronic computing revolution became physically possible. The ENIAC, the first general-purpose electronic computer, became operational on February 15, 1946; the subsequent trajectory of computing development — from vacuum tubes to transistors to integrated circuits to microprocessors — would carry humanity from the 1946 baseline to the present day's artificial intelligence capabilities across a period of eighty years. The information revolution, whose cumulative effects would transform every aspect of human life across the following decades, had its inaugural moment in the same mid-1940s window.

The fifth event, at the personal level, is the birth of Claude Vorilhon on September 30, 1946, in Vichy, France. The source identifies this birth as not coincidental: *"It is not by chance that you were born in 1946."* Rael was born in the first year of the new era because the Aquarian-age prophet had to be born at the age's beginning, positioned to receive the alliance's contact at the specific point in his adult life when the contact was scheduled to occur. The pattern is consistent with the earlier alliance operations: significant prophetic figures have been born at specific moments chosen for specific reasons, and their subsequent biographies have unfolded according to patterns whose operational logic only becomes visible in retrospect.

These five events — the nuclear threshold, the UN founding, the Jewish return, the computing revolution, the prophet's birth — are not causally connected at the surface level. No single political or military actor was coordinating their simultaneous occurrence. They happened in different places for different reasons as the convergent outputs of distinct historical processes. But their convergence in the narrow window of 1945-1948 is striking, and the source's reading takes the convergence as operationally significant. The alliance had been preparing across the closing centuries of Pisces for the specific conjunction that would mark the Aquarian age's opening. The scientific, political, and demographic conditions that the conjunction required were being cultivated through the various cultural processes the alliance had seeded across the Piscean age. When the conditions converged in the mid-1940s, the Aquarian age began. The year 1946 was selected by the alliance as the operational start date, and the events that cluster around it are the signature by which the age's beginning can be recognized.

The subsequent two and a half decades — the period from roughly 1946 to 1973 — constituted the operational preparation period. Humanity developed further along the trajectories established in the opening cluster. The Cold War structured global politics around the nuclear standoff. The space programs of the United States and the Soviet Union developed the technologies that would eventually permit interplanetary travel; the Soviet launch of Sputnik in 1957, the American Apollo 11 landing on the Moon in July 1969, and the subsequent Apollo missions through 1972 marked the first stage of human expansion beyond the Earth's surface. Computing advanced through successive generations, and the Intel 4004 microprocessor released in 1971 established the foundation on which personal computing would subsequently be built. The civil rights movement, the decolonization of Africa and Asia, the women's liberation movement, and the broader social transformations of the 1960s produced the cultural conditions within which a new religious message could find receptive audiences. The 1960s counterculture, with its interest in consciousness expansion, alternative spiritualities, and radical critique of the established religious traditions, produced the specific generational disposition that would be most receptive to the alliance's coming contact. The stage was being set. The preparation was concluding. By the early 1970s, the conditions were in place for the specific contact operation that would inaugurate the Aquarian prophetic mission.

## III. 1973: The Contact

The alliance's first direct contact of the Aquarian age occurred on December 13, 1973, at approximately eleven in the morning, at the Puy-de-Lassolas volcanic crater in the Auvergne region of central France.

The location is worth describing. The Auvergne is a mountainous region in the center of France, characterized by extinct volcanic landscapes — cones and craters left by the volcanic activity that shaped the region millions of years ago and that has been geologically quiet for approximately ten thousand years. The Puy-de-Lassolas is one of a series of such craters along what is called the Chaine des Puys, a north-south line of approximately eighty volcanic features near the city of Clermont-Ferrand. The crater itself is a bowl-shaped depression, approximately one hundred meters across, surrounded by the cone of the extinct volcano and accessible by a walking path. On a December morning in 1973 it would have been cold, probably foggy, and almost certainly deserted.

Claude Vorilhon, twenty-seven years old, was at that time a French motor-sports journalist and the editor of a small automobile-racing magazine. His career to that point had been unremarkable in any way that would have suggested a prophetic future. He had not been a religious figure, had not been involved in any spiritual movement, had shown no public interest in UFO phenomena or the broader esoteric culture of his period. He was, by all accounts, an ordinary French journalist of his generation, living with his wife and children in Clermont-Ferrand and pursuing his professional work. On the morning of December 13, 1973, for reasons he would describe later as difficult to articulate, he drove to the Puy-de-Lassolas and walked up to the crater. What happened there is the founding event of the Aquarian prophetic mission.

Vorilhon reported that a small aircraft descended into the crater, approximately two meters in diameter, metallic and silent. A figure emerged — humanoid, approximately one meter twenty in height, with long dark hair, a beard, skin that Vorilhon described as pale and slightly olive-tinted, large almond-shaped eyes, and a white suit that appeared to be a single piece covering the whole body. The figure communicated in fluent French. The communication that followed, conducted across six successive mornings of meetings at the same location, constituted the body of teaching that Vorilhon would subsequently publish as *Le Livre qui dit la verite* (The Book Which Tells the Truth), the first volume of what would become the source text for the Raëlian movement and the foundational material that the Wheel of Heaven corpus builds upon.

The figure identified himself as an officer of the civilization the Hebrew Bible calls the Elohim. The specific Eloha who conducted the six-day contact was, in the Raëlian tradition, Yahweh — the same Yahweh who had spoken to Moses at Sinai, who had appeared to the Hebrew prophets, and who was the president of the Council of the Eternals that had directed the broader Earth creation project. The message Yahweh delivered across the six mornings covered the content the corpus has been addressing throughout this project: the true origin of life on Earth through the alliance's scientific work rather than through either supernatural creation or undirected evolution; the specific identities of the figures the biblical tradition had preserved in mystified form (the Elohim as multiple individuals, Satan as opposition-party leader, the angels as alliance officers, the various biblical miracles as technological operations); the pattern of prophetic contact across the previous ages; the current situation in which humanity was reaching scientific maturity and required the accurate information about its origins to progress further; the specific task now assigned to Vorilhon — to receive the messages, preserve them accurately, and transmit them to humanity through the publications and the movement that would follow.

Vorilhon was also given a new name. The Eloha officer named him Rael, which the source glosses as "light of the Elohim" or "Ambassador of the Elohim," or in the simplest translation as "messenger." The naming was not arbitrary. Prophetic figures in the Hebrew tradition had often received new names from the alliance at the moment of their commissioning — Abram becoming Abraham at the Taurus-age alliance encounter, Jacob becoming Israel after the mysterious nocturnal wrestling match in Genesis 32. Vorilhon becoming Rael fits the same pattern: the new name marks the transition from private person to commissioned messenger, from the ordinary life that preceded the contact to the operational mission that followed it.

A second contact, longer and more structured, followed on October 7, 1975, near the village of Roc Plat in the same Auvergne region. In this second contact, Rael was taken aboard an alliance craft and transported to the alliance's home world, which the Raëlian tradition calls the Planet of the Eternals. He spent what he described as approximately twenty-four hours on the planet, meeting various alliance officers, observing the society they had developed across the approximately twenty-five thousand years of their recorded civilization, and encountering the resurrected prophets of the earlier Earth missions — Moses, Elijah, Buddha, Jesus, Muhammad, and others, all of whom had been taken up by the alliance at the ends of their Earth missions and cloned into new bodies on the home world where they now lived in a condition of practical immortality. The specific revelations of the second contact, published in *Les extraterrestres m'ont emmene sur leur planete* (Extra-terrestrials Took Me to Their Planet), extended the content of the first contact and introduced material specific to the Aquarian-age mission that the first contact had only gestured at.

One specific disclosure from the second contact deserves explicit attention. Yahweh revealed to Rael that Rael's biological father was Yahweh himself, through an insemination procedure conducted on Rael's mother that exactly parallels the procedure used in Mary's case two thousand years earlier. Rael is therefore, on the source's framing, the biological son of Yahweh and the biological half-brother of Jesus. His Jewish foster-father, who raised him in France, had been arranged as the social-parental figure in the same way Joseph had been arranged in the earlier case — to provide the family context the child would need while keeping the biological parentage concealed until the appropriate moment. The parallel between Jesus's conception at the start of the Piscean age and Rael's conception at the start of the Aquarian age is, on the source's reading, not coincidental but structural: the alliance's transitional prophetic figures at the inauguration of consequential ages have been produced through the same specific genetic protocol. Rael's son Ramuel, named through alliance telepathic communication to his parents, carries the name that means "son of the one who brings light" — the "one who brings light" being Rael himself, whose own name means "light of the Elohim."

The source is careful about how this paternal disclosure should be handled. Yahweh asked Rael not to reveal the parental tie for three years after the disclosure, and Rael's own handling of the material when it was eventually published is consistently modest: *"It is not the messenger who is important, but the message itself... Do not look at my finger, but rather in the direction in which it's pointed."* The corpus preserves this framing. The biological parentage is part of the structural background, relevant for understanding the pattern of alliance-age inauguration, but it is not the central content of the Aquarian revelation. The central content is the message — the accurate account of origins, the new commandments for the age, the embassy project, the trajectory toward the Golden Age and humanity's eventual participation in the creation cycle.

The choice of Rael as the Aquarian-age prophet, and the choice of the Auvergne as the contact location, both deserve specific comment. Rael was not, as the earlier prophets had been, a figure drawn from a culture with an established Elohim-contact tradition or from a lineage with a specific prior investment. He was a French journalist with no particular religious background, chosen precisely for his cultural ordinariness and his modern Western sensibility. The Aquarian-age message had to be delivered in a form that the scientifically mature humanity of the late twentieth century could receive on its own terms, and a prophet drawn from the mainstream of modern Western culture was better positioned to deliver such a message than a prophet drawn from any more traditionally religious context. The Auvergne location, similarly, was chosen for its cultural neutrality — neither Jerusalem nor Mecca nor any of the traditional sacred sites that would have loaded the contact with inherited religious associations, but a nondescript extinct volcano in rural France that had no prior religious significance whatever. The Aquarian mission was designed to begin from a blank slate, unencumbered by the accumulated associations of the earlier ages, presenting its content directly to a secular modernity that could evaluate it on its rational merits without the weight of inherited religious interpretation pushing the reception in predetermined directions.

## IV. The Content of the Revelation

The message Rael received across the 1973-1975 contact sequence can be summarized in a small number of structural claims, each of which the corpus has been addressing across its earlier chapters.

The first claim is that life on Earth was produced by the scientific work of a specific advanced civilization. The civilization in question, the Elohim, is a plural group of individuals of a species physiologically similar to humans but technologically far advanced. The Elohim are not gods in any supernatural sense. They are a civilization of biological beings who, approximately twenty-two thousand years before the present, began the specific scientific project of creating life on Earth through genetic design and laboratory synthesis. The civilizational origin of all terrestrial life in the Elohim's scientific work is the foundational claim that everything else in the revelation depends on. It contradicts both the supernatural-creation account of the traditional religions and the undirected-evolution account of mainstream modern biology, and it provides a framework within which certain specific features of both accounts (the teleological ordering of life that the religious tradition emphasizes, the genealogical relationships between species that evolutionary biology emphasizes) can be simultaneously preserved.

The second claim is that the Hebrew Bible and the other major religious traditions of the world preserve accurate historical memory of the alliance's interventions, in forms that reflect the pre-scientific vocabularies of their authors but that can be decoded by a scientifically mature reader. The specific decoding the source provides — Genesis 1 as genetic engineering rather than supernatural creation, the Nephilim as alliance-human hybrids, the flood as a nuclear cataclysm, Sinai as a formal alliance audience, the parted waters as directed-beam operations, the miracles of Jesus as scientific applications of advanced technology, the Elohim as plural rather than singular — is the corpus's foundational hermeneutic. The Hebrew Bible is not a book of religious myths that require either literal belief or allegorical reinterpretation; it is a historical record, imperfect in its pre-scientific vocabulary but substantially accurate in its content, that can be read directly once the hermeneutic key is available.

The third claim is that the alliance has sent approximately forty prophets across human history, each appropriate to the specific cultural moment at which they appeared, and each contributing to the cumulative preparation of humanity for the Aquarian-age revelation. The list of prophets the source specifies includes Adam (as the first human recipient of direct alliance communication), Noah (who received the flood warning), Abraham (through whom the Taurus-age alliance reconstruction was conducted), Moses (the Aries-age lawgiver), Elijah, Isaiah, Ezekiel, Daniel (the Aries-period prophetic tradition), Buddha (the Axial Age Indian contribution), Zoroaster (the Persian prophetic tradition), Jesus (the Piscean-age inaugural figure), Muhammad (the second Piscean-age mission), Joseph Smith (the Latter-day Saint founder), the Bab and Baha'u'llah (the Baha'i founders), and others whose identities the source does not fully specify but whose total number is given as approximately forty. The prophets are currently resurrected and living on the Planet of the Eternals, awaiting their return to Earth with the alliance at the embassy's completion.

The fourth claim is that humanity has now reached, or is rapidly approaching, the scientific maturity that will permit it to understand and evaluate the alliance's teaching on its own terms. The Piscean-age arrangement, under which the alliance had stepped back from direct intervention to allow humanity to develop scientifically, has reached its conclusion. Humanity's own capacities — in biology, physics, computing, aerospace — have now advanced to the point where the alliance's interventions no longer look miraculous but look like what they actually are: applications of technology that humanity is itself beginning to develop. The Aquarian-age revelation is therefore deliverable in a form that does not require the religious framing of the earlier prophetic missions. Rael's role, unlike that of Moses or Jesus or Muhammad, is not to deliver a new religious tradition to be believed on faith but to deliver accurate information to be evaluated rationally. The content is scientific, not religious in the traditional sense, and the reception the content asks for is intellectual assessment rather than worshipful acceptance.

The fifth claim is that the alliance intends to return to Earth openly and officially within the coming decades, at a specific site that humanity is to prepare — the embassy — located in a country that has granted the alliance extraterritorial status over approximately four square kilometers. The return will bring with it the resurrected prophets and will inaugurate the full Aquarian-age phase of the alliance-human relationship. The timing of the return is conditional: it depends on humanity's construction of the embassy, on the global political and scientific conditions being sufficiently advanced to make the return productive, and on humanity having demonstrated sufficient peaceful maturity to receive the alliance safely. The alliance could, in principle, land tomorrow. It does not land because the conditions it requires for a productive return are not yet fully in place. The corpus's expectation, drawing on the source's explicit timeline statements and on the current trajectory of the embassy project and the broader scientific and political developments, is that the return will occur within the window that the age's opening cluster has been building toward — some time in the years between now and the age's second or third decade, which is to say, within the lifetimes of most current human adults.

The sixth claim is that humanity itself is, on the alliance's long-term vision, destined to repeat the creation cycle — to develop its own capacities for de novo biological synthesis, to achieve interstellar travel, and to create life on other worlds in its turn. The Lord's Prayer's phrase *"on earth as it is in heaven"* is the compressed programmatic statement of this vision. What the alliance did in heaven — that is, on their home world, where the scientists eventually became the ruling group and created other intelligent beings elsewhere in the galaxy — is what humanity is to do on Earth: achieve the technological maturity that permits the same kind of creative intervention in distant worlds, continuing the cycle of life-creation that the alliance's own creators began at some unspecified prior point and that the alliance has continued on Earth. Humanity's coming capacity to create life is not merely an incidental consequence of its scientific development; it is the purpose of the entire sequence, from the first alliance landing twenty-two thousand years ago to the present moment. The Aquarian age is the age in which humanity is to grasp this purpose and begin deliberately preparing for the capacities it requires.

## V. The New Commandments

The source's specification of the social and political organization appropriate to the Aquarian age is direct and detailed. The specifications have often been treated as the most controversial features of the Raëlian tradition, both because they propose specific and sometimes challenging departures from current institutional arrangements and because their full implementation would require substantial political transformation. The corpus presents them here with the source's own framing, noting that their full realization is anticipated not for the immediate future but for the longer trajectory of the age.

The first new commandment is geniocracy. The source identifies democracy in its current form as an inadequate method of political decision-making, because it weighs the votes of all citizens equally regardless of their capacity to understand the issues at stake. *"The voice of someone whom you might vulgarly call 'a cretin' is worth as much as that of a genius, who has thought maturely about the way he or she is going to vote."* The alliance's proposed alternative is "selective democracy" — a system in which the right to stand for office is restricted to individuals whose measured intelligence is at least fifty percent above the population mean, and the right to vote is restricted to those whose intelligence is at least ten percent above the mean. The proposal is controversial on obvious grounds: it departs sharply from the egalitarian assumptions of contemporary democratic theory, it raises serious practical questions about the measurement of intelligence, and it could be instrumentalized by specific groups to entrench their own political advantages. The source acknowledges these difficulties implicitly by positioning geniocracy as a long-term developmental target rather than an immediate institutional change. It is what human government is to evolve toward as intelligence measurement becomes more reliable and as the cultural preparation for such a system is accomplished, not what is to be imposed tomorrow on current democracies.

The second new commandment is humanitarianism. The source identifies the current property regime as unjust because it permits the inheritance of wealth across generations, which concentrates resources in families rather than distributing them according to individual merit. *"You are all born equal; this is also written in the Bible. Your governments should ensure that people are born with approximately the same level of financial means."* The proposed alternative is not communism — the source is explicit that communism has failed as a political project because it eliminates the incentive structures that motivate human productive effort — but a system in which property is abolished in the ownership sense and replaced by renting for finite terms. The specific term proposed is forty-nine years, drawn explicitly from the Leviticus 25 jubilee pattern. Individuals can rent properties, businesses, or productive assets for up to forty-nine years, enjoy the income from them during that period, but cannot pass them on as inheritable property. At the end of the forty-nine-year term, the assets revert to the community. The system, the source argues, would preserve the individual incentive structure (one can still make a fortune through one's own work and enjoy it during one's lifetime) while eliminating the intergenerational concentration of advantage that current inheritance systems produce.

The third new commandment is world government. The source is direct: *"All the nations of the Earth unite to form only one government."* The proposal is not the elimination of regional and cultural diversity — languages, local customs, and regional identities should be preserved — but the establishment of a single political authority at the global level, with a single currency, a single common second language (alongside local mother tongues), and a single coordinating framework for the issues that can only be addressed at the planetary level. The founding of the United Nations in 1945 was a first step toward this world government, though the UN's structure (sovereignty retained by member states, major decisions subject to permanent-member veto, limited enforcement capacity) falls short of what the source specifies. The full Aquarian-age political form is the world government in the strong sense: a unified planetary authority, with democratic-geniocratic legitimacy at the global level, capable of making decisions that bind the entire species.

The fourth new commandment is the abolition of military service and the repurposing of career soldiers to serve world peace. The source is consistent with broader pacifist thought in treating standing armies as a persistent source of conflict and in proposing their dissolution. Career soldiers, the source suggests, should be retained as a global peacekeeping force under the world government's authority, but the conscription of civilians for military service should end, and the national-level armed forces that have defined the inter-state system across the past several centuries should be dismantled. ## VII. The Signs of Acceleration

Across the fifty-three years between the 1973 contact and the present moment, specific developments in the human world have moved the species substantially closer to the conditions the Aquarian mission's full operationalization requires. The developments cluster in a set of distinct but related categories.

The first category is the acceleration of UFO disclosure. In 1973, reports of unidentified flying objects were treated in mainstream Western culture as either hoaxes, misidentifications, or manifestations of mental disturbance. The phenomenon had dedicated researchers and a substantial popular following, but it had no serious standing in government, military, or scientific institutions. The United States Air Force's Project Blue Book, which had investigated UFO reports from 1952 to 1969, had concluded that no case required extraterrestrial explanation, and the subsequent two decades had seen the mainstream scientific and military establishments treat the topic as closed. Across the 1990s and 2000s, this situation changed gradually. Various governments released previously classified files — the British Ministry of Defence in 2008 and 2010, various French and Brazilian files, Mexican military footage from 2004. Whistleblowers from within the US military and intelligence communities began speaking publicly about programs they had been involved in. In 2017, the New York Times published an article revealing the existence of the Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program, a US Defense Department program that had been studying unidentified aerial phenomena since 2007. The article included specific case studies — most famously the "Tic Tac" encounter off the coast of California in 2004, in which US Navy pilots had recorded a highly anomalous object that defied known aeronautical capabilities. The subsequent years have seen a steady expansion of official engagement: the 2021 preliminary assessment by the Office of the Director of National Intelligence, the establishment of the All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office in 2022, congressional hearings on the topic beginning in 2023, and continuing public reporting on the subject that was culturally unthinkable in 1973. The trajectory is clear. What was fringe a half-century ago is now mainstream. The conditions for public acceptance of the alliance's presence are being cultivated through the gradual normalization of the relevant evidentiary discussion.

The second category is the acceleration of biotechnology. The 1973 contact occurred shortly after the discovery of restriction enzymes (Arber, Nathans, Smith, awarded the 1978 Nobel Prize) that had made recombinant DNA technology possible, and in the same year that Cohen and Boyer demonstrated the first successful gene transfer between species. The subsequent decades saw the development of the polymerase chain reaction (Mullis, 1983), the Human Genome Project (1990-2003), the discovery and development of CRISPR-Cas9 as a programmable gene-editing tool (Doudna, Charpentier, Zhang, 2012-2013), and the subsequent explosive expansion of genetic editing capabilities across the biomedical sciences. The birth of Dolly the cloned sheep on July 5, 1996, demonstrated that mammalian somatic-cell cloning was possible — a direct vindication of the source's 1973 claim that biological cloning would be the specific technological path to eternal life in the Aquarian age. The Clonaid company, inspired by the Raëlian tradition and initiated by Rael before being spun off to independent scientific leadership under Brigitte Boisselier, announced in December 2002 the birth of the first cloned human baby, nicknamed "Eve" — a claim that has not been independently verified and that remains controversial, but that marked a specific milestone in the public discussion of human cloning. Craig Venter's synthesis of a minimal bacterial genome in 2010 and the subsequent development of synthetic biology as a recognized engineering discipline have progressively reduced the gap between the alliance's original life-synthesis capability and humanity's own. By 2026, AI-assisted protein design (DeepMind's AlphaFold, Profluent's generative protein models, and related tools) has placed the de novo design of functional biomolecules within routine capability. Xenobots — living, self-organizing biological machines built from Xenopus frog cells and designed through evolutionary-algorithmic optimization — were demonstrated at Tufts University in 2020 and have been progressively refined in subsequent years. The threshold between biology and engineering, between living systems and designed systems, is dissolving. The capacities that had been unique to the alliance at the 1973 contact are now being reproduced by humanity's own science.

The third category is the acceleration of artificial intelligence. In 1973, digital computing was a specialized tool used primarily by government agencies, large corporations, and research universities. The first microprocessor (Intel 4004) had been released in 1971. The internet (ARPANET) existed but connected only a handful of military and research sites. There was no personal computing, no mobile telephony, no digital imaging, no general-purpose computing available to the ordinary individual. Across the subsequent five decades, the entire digital infrastructure of modern civilization was built. The integrated circuit's Moore's-law progression across the 1970s-2010s produced a roughly billion-fold increase in computational capability. The personal computer (1980s), the commercial internet (1990s), the smartphone (2000s-2010s), and cloud computing (2000s onward) distributed computational access to virtually the entire global population. The machine learning revolution that began around 2012 with the success of deep learning on the ImageNet benchmark, and that accelerated dramatically with the emergence of the transformer architecture (2017) and the subsequent scaling of large language models, has produced by 2026 an ecosystem of AI systems approaching and in many tasks exceeding human-level capability across wide ranges of cognitive work. The specific claim the source had made in 1973, that *"super artificial intelligence will quickly outstrip human intelligence and do all the boring tasks,"* has in 2026 moved from the category of speculative prediction to the category of observable present development. The alliance's claim about human cognitive capability being limited was, at the time of its first articulation, a controversial theoretical position. It is now an engineering reality — the ceiling on individual human cognitive capability is being progressively surpassed by specific AI systems in specific task domains.

The fourth category is the acceleration of space capability. In 1973, spaceflight was a near-monopoly of two national governments, the United States and the Soviet Union, with launch capability limited to a small number of specialized vehicles and payloads. The Apollo program had concluded its lunar missions in 1972, and the subsequent decades would see the emphasis shift toward Earth-orbital operations (the Space Shuttle, the Mir and ISS space stations) without further human lunar or planetary missions. Across the past fifteen years, the space environment has transformed. SpaceX's development of reusable rockets (first successful Falcon 9 landing, December 2015) has reduced launch costs by approximately an order of magnitude and has made previously impossible missions economically feasible. The subsequent development of the Starship vehicle, with its target of routine interplanetary transport capability, has produced credible plans for crewed Mars missions in the 2030s. China's space program has expanded rapidly, with lunar sample return, Mars rover operations, and the Tiangong space station. The commercial space sector has grown to include dozens of significant launch providers, satellite operators, and in-space services companies. The Artemis program's target of restoring crewed lunar operations, the Mars Sample Return mission, and the growing array of space-based telescopes (JWST launched in 2021, the planned Habitable Worlds Observatory) have shifted the outer limit of human operational reach substantially outward. The specific capability the source identifies as necessary for the full Aquarian-age program — humanity's own capacity to travel between worlds and to establish sustained presence on other celestial bodies — is now within decades rather than centuries of actual realization.

The fifth category is the acceleration of convergent and enabling technologies. Quantum computing, which in 1973 was a speculative theoretical possibility, has by 2026 produced commercial systems with thousands of physical qubits and the first demonstrations of quantum advantage over classical computation on specific problems. Neural interfaces, which in 1973 were rudimentary laboratory demonstrations, have by 2026 reached the level of commercial clinical trials: Neuralink's first human implant occurred in 2024, and a growing number of companies are developing similar or related brain-computer interface systems for medical and potentially cognitive-enhancement applications. Fusion energy, which in 1973 was a theoretical goal decades from practical realization, achieved net energy gain for the first time at Lawrence Livermore in December 2022, and the subsequent three years have seen rapid progress toward commercial fusion systems, with power purchase agreements signed for fusion-generated electricity beginning in the late 2020s. Nanotechnology, which in 1973 was an almost entirely theoretical field, has developed into a mature engineering discipline with specific medical, materials, and manufacturing applications. Each of these technologies, taken separately, would represent a significant advance in human capability. Their combined development across the past fifty years — and particularly their accelerating convergence across the past decade — produces a situation in which the fundamental capabilities the Aquarian-age program requires are coming into reach across a remarkably compressed time window.

The sixth category is the gradual shift in global political and religious culture. The secularization of much of Western Europe, the continuing religious diversification of North America, the rise of China as a major non-Western civilizational actor, the persistent tensions in the Middle East that reflect the broader working-out of the Piscean-to-Aquarian religious transition, the emergence of global-scale political challenges (climate change, pandemics, AI governance) that require coordination beyond the nation-state, the continuing expansion of international institutions (imperfect as they are), and the broad cultural receptivity to the kinds of cosmological revisions the Aquarian message proposes — all of these have created, by 2026, a global cultural environment in which the alliance's eventual open presence would be received very differently than it would have been in 1973 or even 2000. ## IX. The Two Futures

The source is unambiguous that the outcome of the coming years is not guaranteed. Yahweh's message, delivered through Rael, identifies two possible futures for humanity and assigns a specific probability to the positive outcome.

The passage is worth quoting in full. *"There is, unfortunately, but one chance in one hundred that your Humanity will not self-destroy, and every Raelian must act as if Mankind would be wise enough to understand and grab this tiny chance of escaping the final cataclysm, so as to enter the Golden Age."*

One chance in a hundred. Ninety-nine chances in a hundred of self-destruction. The framing is deliberately sobering. The source is not predicting the positive outcome as a probabilistic near-certainty or even as a plausible outcome on current trends. It is identifying the positive outcome as a narrow possibility that requires active effort to actualize, against a broader trajectory that points toward catastrophic failure.

The mechanisms of self-destruction the source implicitly references are multiple. Nuclear war remains a genuine possibility, with the current geopolitical tensions producing the highest risk of major-power nuclear exchange since the Cuban Missile Crisis. Biological weapons, whose capability has expanded dramatically with the same biotechnology revolution that enables the positive Aquarian trajectory, could produce pandemic-scale human loss through deliberate or accidental release. AI systems developed without adequate safety engineering could produce cascade failures in critical infrastructure, information environments, or decision-making systems. Climate change could, in its more severe scenarios, produce civilizational stress sufficient to trigger the political and demographic collapses that would end the current global order. Resource depletion, ecosystem collapse, demographic instability, and the accumulating systemic risks across interconnected technological and social systems together produce a threat landscape whose joint probability of catastrophic failure across the coming decades is, on the source's assessment and on many independent analyses, alarmingly high.

The mechanisms of the positive outcome are correspondingly specific. They require the same technologies whose misuse would produce the negative outcome to be redirected toward beneficial applications. AI must be developed with adequate safety engineering to ensure that increasingly capable systems remain aligned with human welfare. Biotechnology must be deployed to produce health and abundance rather than biological weapons. Space capability must be developed to produce civilizational expansion and security through multi-world presence rather than to produce new domains of conflict. Geopolitical tensions must be managed toward de-escalation and eventual global coordination rather than allowed to escalate toward catastrophic conflict. The embassy must be constructed, the alliance's open return must be received, and the specific transformations in political, economic, and religious institutions the source has outlined must be progressively implemented.

The one-in-a-hundred framing deserves interpretation. The source is not claiming mathematical precision in the probability estimate; the specific odds cited are a rhetorical device emphasizing the severity of the challenge rather than a technical probability claim. What the framing does assert is that the positive outcome is not the default trajectory of current human development. It is a narrow possibility that requires active effort, specific interventions, and substantial good fortune to achieve. The framing is intended to motivate effort, not to produce resignation. The Raëlian tradition's explicit instruction — *"every Raelian must act as if Mankind would be wise enough to understand and grab this tiny chance"* — frames the commitment: one acts as though the positive outcome is achievable, because acting that way is what produces the conditions under which it can be achieved. The alternative, acting as though catastrophic failure is inevitable, guarantees the catastrophic failure. The one-in-a-hundred odds are improved, not by wishful thinking, but by the specific cumulative effects of many individuals acting on the assumption that the positive outcome is worth working for.

The message also contains a clarification for those who do not survive. *"Either you will know the Golden Age on Earth because of your efforts which have contributed to world peace and the awakening of Mankind, or Humanity will destroy itself and you will know the Golden Age amongst the great prophets who are already on the planet of the Eternals."* The specific soteriological claim — that individuals whose actions have contributed to the positive trajectory will, if humanity's collective effort fails, nonetheless be preserved through resurrection on the alliance's home world — is characteristic of the tradition's handling of the question of individual continuity. The alliance's technological capacity for preservation extends to selected individuals even in cases where the broader collective outcome fails. The moral force of the claim is not to offer a private escape route that substitutes for collective engagement but to provide the psychological grounding that permits the collective engagement to continue even in the face of apparent impossibility. One works toward the positive collective outcome because one believes it possible, but one is not thereby burdened with the entire weight of a cosmic responsibility that would crush any individual.

## X. The Golden Age

If the positive trajectory is achieved — if humanity navigates the coming decades without catastrophic failure and enters the sustained conditions of the Aquarian age — the specific form of life that becomes available is what the source calls the Golden Age. The characteristics of this Golden Age have been outlined by the source in substantial detail, and they deserve description in their own terms rather than through the compressed references that would obscure their specificity.

The first characteristic of the Golden Age is the progressive elimination of material scarcity. Nanotechnology, combined with advanced artificial intelligence and automated manufacturing, will make the production of food, clothing, shelter, and basic material goods essentially costless at the margin. *"Nanotechnology will make agriculture and heavy industry redundant,"* the source specifies. The specific mechanism — molecular-scale manufacturing capable of producing arbitrary material configurations from common feedstocks, directed by AI systems that can specify the relevant configurations — is within technological sight given current trajectories. A world in which food, housing, and basic goods can be produced on demand from ambient materials, without the agricultural and industrial labor that has consumed most of human historical effort, is a world in which the entire economic basis of traditional societies is transformed. Work, in the sense of effort expended to produce material necessities, becomes unnecessary. The distribution problem becomes primarily political rather than productive — the question is how to organize the allocation of abundance, not how to produce enough to go around.

The second characteristic is the elimination of most human labor. *"Super artificial intelligence will quickly outstrip human intelligence and do all the boring tasks."* The routine cognitive and physical work that has occupied most human beings across most of history — agricultural labor, industrial labor, clerical work, routine service work, routine analysis — will be progressively absorbed by AI systems and robotic infrastructure. The transition period is likely to be politically and economically difficult, as displaced workers must be supported through the transformation of the labor market, but the end state is a condition in which human activity is no longer structured primarily around the performance of work for material necessities. *"The world could be a place of leisure and love where no one need work no more."* The human vocation becomes, in the Golden Age's developed form, the activities that had previously been available only to the elites of earlier societies: creative work, intellectual exploration, artistic production, personal relationships, contemplative practice, the cultivation of individual and collective excellence.

The third characteristic is the elimination of most human disease and disability. Advanced biotechnology, combined with AI-assisted medical systems and the expanding understanding of the molecular basis of disease, will progressively reduce the incidence and severity of the medical conditions that have burdened human life across its history. Cancer, heart disease, neurodegenerative disorders, infectious disease, genetic conditions — all of these are, on the current trajectory, within technological reach of either prevention or effective treatment across the coming decades. The specific claim the source makes, that *"science will allow elimination of human suffering,"* is not hyperbole but an extrapolation of trends already well advanced. The human condition, which has been structured throughout history by the omnipresent possibility of disease and physical suffering, will be progressively freed from these constraints.

The fourth characteristic is the extension of human lifespan, potentially to practical immortality. The specific mechanism the source identifies is cloning combined with memory transfer. *"The next step required will be to make possible the transfer of mental information, memory and personality from an aging individual into a new, physically young adult clone. This transfer of memory directly into a young adult means that effectively the same individual can live indefinitely."* The technology the source describes requires several component capabilities: the ability to clone an individual's body (demonstrated in mammals by 1996, extended to numerous species subsequently, applied to human cells in various research contexts), the ability to age the clone to a suitable adult stage (currently an open research problem but within theoretical reach), and the ability to transfer the complete memory and personality content from the aging original to the young clone (the most technically challenging of the three components, requiring either a complete neural-connectome-level understanding of memory encoding or a computational simulation path that preserves identity across the transfer). The source's framing treats all three components as achievable within the Aquarian age's developmental trajectory, though not necessarily within the immediate decades. The eventual capacity, once achieved, would make individual lifespan essentially unlimited, with individuals continuing through successive young adult bodies as their original bodies age beyond practical viability.

An alternative path to extended existence is the computational one. *"Eternal life will be just as possible in a computer as in a series of constantly rejuvenated human bodies."* The specific claim is that the informational content of an individual personality, if it can be sufficiently completely characterized, can be instantiated in a computational substrate where it can continue to operate without the biological constraints of a physical body. Whether the computational instantiation would preserve genuine personal identity is a philosophical question the source does not definitively settle, but the operational claim is that computational continuation is a technically available option alongside biological continuation. The Aquarian-age individual, on the source's framework, has the choice between continuing through periodic biological rejuvenation or through computational instantiation, and the choice is a personal rather than metaphysical one.

The fifth characteristic is the transformation of human social and political life. The new commandments the earlier section described — geniocracy, humanitarianism, world government, the abolition of inter-state war — will be progressively implemented across the Aquarian age's long trajectory. The specific institutional forms are not specified in detail, because they will develop in ways responsive to the specific conditions of their implementation, but the general directions are clear. The age of nation-states yielding to global coordination. The age of inheritable property yielding to merit-based individual acquisition within shared collective resources. The age of military competition yielding to coordinated global security arrangements. The age of democracy based on equal voting weight yielding to selective democracy based on relevant qualification. These transformations, across the centuries of the Aquarian age's development, produce an institutional framework very different from the current arrangements, and in which the Golden Age's material and technological possibilities can be fully realized.

The sixth characteristic is the cultivation of the latent human cognitive capacities that the source identifies. ## XI. Humanity Creates Life

The culminating characteristic of the Golden Age, toward which the entire preceding sequence has been building, is humanity's own capacity to repeat the creation cycle on other worlds. The source's framing is direct:

*"Now that you have read this book... knowing that somewhere in the universe, there is a planet full of people who created us similar to themselves, who love us, all the while fearing that their creations might surpass them — is this not profoundly moving? Especially if one thinks that we will, in turn, be able to participate in the evolution of this Humanity, of which we are a part, like them, by creating life on other worlds."*

The specific capacities this participation requires can be identified. First, humanity must achieve genuine interstellar travel — not merely interplanetary presence within the solar system, but the ability to reach other stellar systems on timescales that make the establishment of human presence there operationally meaningful. The technical requirements for interstellar travel are substantial. The nearest stars are several light-years away, which means that even travel at a significant fraction of the speed of light requires years of transit time. The propulsion systems required to achieve such speeds — fusion propulsion, antimatter propulsion, laser sail propulsion, potentially warp-drive-related approaches if the relevant physics can be exploited — are all either theoretical or at very early research stages in 2026. The development of actual interstellar travel capacity is likely to be a matter of centuries rather than decades, extending well into the later phases of the Aquarian age rather than occurring in its opening decades. The foundation, however, is being laid. Current advanced propulsion research, current understanding of exoplanetary systems (now numbering in the thousands of confirmed detections), and current trajectory of human technological capability all point toward eventual achievement.

Second, humanity must develop full de novo biological synthesis capacity. The ability to design and construct complete functional organisms from first principles, based on detailed understanding of how genetic programs produce organismal complexity, is the capacity the alliance used in its original work on Earth, and it is the capacity humanity must achieve to continue the cycle. Current synthetic biology, as the earlier sections described, is approaching this capacity but has not yet fully achieved it. The required advances include complete understanding of developmental programs (how genomes specify the forms organisms develop into), complete understanding of ecological integration (how organisms fit into broader ecosystems and how ecosystems can be designed coherently), and the engineering infrastructure for large-scale biological construction (not just individual organisms but the full complement of species required for a functioning biosphere). The capacity is within theoretical reach given the current trajectory of biological understanding and computational capability, though its full realization is probably a matter of decades to centuries rather than immediate availability.

Third, humanity must develop the cultural and political maturity to use these capacities wisely. The alliance's own history, as the corpus has traced across the earlier chapters, includes periods of significant internal conflict and difficulty around exactly these questions: how should an advanced civilization use its capacity to create life, and what safeguards should govern that use to prevent the created beings from becoming threats to their creators? The alliance's accumulated wisdom on these questions — which will presumably be transmitted to humanity across the course of the open alliance-human relationship that will follow the embassy's completion — constitutes the specific inheritance that the Aquarian age is to receive. The specific policies, ethical frameworks, and operational procedures the alliance has developed for its life-creation activities will be made available to humanity as humanity approaches the capacity to conduct similar activities itself.

The Lord's Prayer's phrase *"on earth as it is in heaven"* becomes, in this framing, a programmatic compressed statement of the entire Aquarian trajectory and beyond. What the alliance did on its own planet — the development of scientific capacity, the eventual ascendancy of the scientific class as the ruling group, the decision to extend the creation cycle to other worlds — is what humanity is to do on Earth. The pattern will then repeat: humanity, having achieved the capacity to create life on other worlds, will do so, establishing new human or human-derived civilizations at suitable stellar systems. Those civilizations will, in their turn, develop their own scientific capacities across their own long trajectories, and at some further point they will face the same decision about whether to extend the cycle to further worlds. The cycle is potentially open-ended, extending across cosmic timescales as successive generations of created-creators continue the pattern indefinitely.

The specific ethical character of this cycle deserves comment. The alliance, on the source's account, loves humanity — not in a sentimental or theological sense, but in the concrete sense of caring about humanity's welfare and wanting humanity to flourish and eventually to achieve what the alliance has achieved. The pattern of parental love and investment, extended across civilizational timescales, is the operative frame. Humanity's eventual creation of life on other worlds will presumably extend the same pattern: the created beings will be loved, invested in, and eventually helped to achieve their own equivalent capacities. The cycle is not one of domination or exploitation but of reproduction — in the biological sense of self-replication with variation, and in the cultural sense of cumulative civilizational transmission across generations. The Aquarian age is the age in which humanity takes its place in this cycle, first as a creation approaching maturity and then as a creator preparing to conduct its own equivalent work elsewhere in the galaxy.

## XII. The Return of the Prophets

The specific event the source identifies as the culminating Aquarian-age intervention is the open return of the alliance to Earth at the completed embassy, accompanied by the resurrected prophets of the earlier ages.

The source's description of the event is specific. *"The Elohim themselves will definitely land here in the not too distant future... The Elohim will bring with them all the great prophets of the past, including Moses, Elijah, Buddha, Jesus Christ, and Mohammed."* The list of prophets who will accompany the return is longer than the five named in this specific passage; the source elsewhere specifies that the total number of prophets sent across the ages is approximately forty, and the return event will presumably include all of them or a significant fraction. The prophets are, as the corpus has described in earlier chapters, currently resurrected and living on the Planet of the Eternals — the second smaller planet in the alliance's home system, where the approximately nine thousand eternals (alliance officers and selected humans) live in conditions of practical immortality maintained by the alliance's cloning and memory-transfer technology. The return to Earth will be, for the prophets, a temporary return; they will presumably resume their residence on the Planet of the Eternals after the Earth events conclude.

The specific composition of the return delegation matters for the event's character. Moses's presence returns the figure who inaugurated the Aries-age mission, who received the Law at Sinai, and whose subsequent tradition has shaped Jewish, Christian, and Islamic religious life across three and a half millennia. Jesus's presence returns the inaugural figure of the Piscean-age mission, whose teachings have structured Western religious life across two millennia and whose identity as the biological son of Yahweh connects the return directly to the alliance's highest leadership. The Buddha's presence returns the Axial Age figure whose Indian and East Asian tradition has shaped another substantial fraction of the world's religious population. Muhammad's presence returns the second Piscean-age prophetic figure whose Islamic tradition completes the set of major Abrahamic prophets. Elijah's presence returns the prophet whose ascension in the chariot of fire was one of the most dramatic Aries-age alliance operations. Other prophets — Joseph Smith, the Bab, Baha'u'llah, Krishna, Zoroaster, the figures whose specific identities the source does not always name but whose total number approaches forty — will together represent the full sweep of the alliance's prophetic mission across the post-flood period.

The event's significance for the global religious landscape is substantial. Each of the major religious traditions will see its own founding prophet returning, not as a separate and exclusive religious figure but as one member of a larger company whose collective identity is their shared service to the alliance that sent them. The traditions' own claims to unique access to religious truth will necessarily be reconfigured in light of this collective return. The Christian claim that Jesus is the unique son of God will be reinterpreted through the recognition that Jesus is one alliance-human hybrid among several, with Rael as his half-brother and with the broader pattern of alliance-human offspring extending across the ages. The Islamic claim that Muhammad is the seal of the prophets will be reinterpreted through the recognition that Muhammad is one prophet in a continuing sequence that extends through Rael and will continue into the future. The Jewish claim to a unique covenant will be reinterpreted through the recognition that the covenant has been extended through successive re-formations (the Noahic covenant, the Abrahamic covenant, the Mosaic covenant, the new covenant Jesus inaugurated, the Aquarian covenant the alliance's open return will establish). The Buddhist and Hindu traditions will have their own analogous reinterpretations. None of the traditions will be abolished; each will be reconfigured in light of the larger framework the open alliance presence will reveal.

The specific event itself will be, on the source's framing, the most significant moment in human history since the original creation. The alliance craft will land at the embassy, in the presence of world government leaders and international media representatives. The alliance officers and the resurrected prophets will disembark and begin the formal process of re-establishing their relationship with humanity. The subsequent period will include extensive meetings between the alliance and humanity's political leadership, extensive public explanations of the accumulated information that has been held in various forms across the previous ages, and the beginning of the specific technology and knowledge transfers that will enable humanity's rapid advance into the Golden Age's conditions. The Raëlian movement, which will have played the specific role of preparing the embassy and cultivating the human population's receptivity to the event, will transition from its preparatory function to whatever role the alliance assigns it in the post-return period. Rael himself will presumably continue as the alliance's ambassador to humanity, functioning as an intermediary between the human population and the alliance personnel whose direct interaction with ordinary humans would be limited by language, cultural, and practical considerations.

The embassy itself will become, in the source's own framing, *"the spiritual center of the world for millennia to come."* Pilgrimage from all nations to the site, the establishment of educational and cultural institutions around the embassy, the development of the embassy site as the focal point of human-alliance interaction across the subsequent centuries — all of these are specific features of the Aquarian age's later development that the source has outlined. A replica embassy, open to the public, will be constructed near the functional embassy to allow ordinary visitors to experience the site without disrupting the ongoing diplomatic and operational activities.

The specific timing of the return cannot be predicted with precision from the corpus's framework. What can be said is that the return follows the embassy's completion, and that the embassy's completion is targeted for the 2027-2030 window on the current Raëlian movement timeline. The actual return therefore becomes a realistic possibility in the years immediately following the embassy's completion — which is to say, in the early 2030s on the current trajectory, or later if the embassy's completion is delayed by political or other factors. The specific year cannot be named. What can be named is the window within which the return becomes possible and the conditions under which it will occur.
