## I. The Age Itself

The eleventh age is the age of the fish, the age of the virgin, and the age during which the alliance's message at last began to reach the wider world the Hebrews had failed to reach.

The Age of Pisces runs from –210 to 1,950, a span of 2,160 years, following immediately upon the Age of Aries. It is the first age of the corpus whose entire span falls within what the conventional historical record can document in detail. Everything a Western-educated person thinks of when they think of "the past two thousand years" belongs to the Age of Pisces. What the Wheel of Heaven framework offers for this period is not a parallel history but a deeper context — the political and religious structure within which these visible events make a different kind of sense.

The age is named for its constellation: *Pisces*, the Fishes. The ram-cults of Aries gave way to a new religious symbolism organized around the figure of the fish — most famously the *ichthys* symbol of early Christianity. The symbolism of Pisces is never only Piscean: the precessional ages encode their signatures in doubled form — both the current constellation and its opposite across the zodiac. The constellation opposite Pisces is Virgo, the Virgin. A Piscean-age religious tradition whose symbolism referenced both the fish and the virgin would preserve the precessional signature with doubled strength. Christianity preserves exactly this: Jesus surrounded by fishermen and fish-imagery; his mother Mary, the Virgin. The fish and the virgin are the age's primary religious signs, both astronomically encoded, both preserved across two thousand years.

## II. The Preparation and the Conception

The source describes the alliance's strategic decision directly: *"They decided to send a Messiah who would be able to communicate worldwide what the people of Israel were then the only ones to know."*

The conception operation is described in specific terms: *"The creators therefore decided to arrange for a child to be born of a woman of the Earth and one of their own people. The child in question would thereby inherit certain telepathic faculties, which humans lack."* Jesus is a specific kind of biological hybrid — the child of a human mother and an Eloha father. The biological inheritance included telepathic capabilities that would distinguish Jesus's mental operation from that of ordinary humans.

The birth received alliance attention. The "star in the east" that guided the magi is read as *"one of the spacecrafts of the creators"* serving as a navigation beacon.

## III. The Initiation

The baptism is read operationally: *"When he came of age, Jesus was led to the creators, so that they could reveal to him his true identity, introduce him to his father, reveal his mission and make known to him various scientific techniques."*

The forty-day desert testing by Satan follows. The source's reading: *"The devil, 'Satan', the creator of whom we spoke previously, was always convinced that nothing good could come of humanity on Earth. So he tested Jesus to find out if his intelligence was positive, and if he really loved and respected his creators."* Satan is performing the function of adversarial interviewer — testing the prospective prophet's commitment through escalating challenges. The Greek word *diabolos*, from which "devil" derives, means simply "slanderer." The source notes: *"What is the word for slanderer in Greek? Simply diablos. Here is our famous devil, but he still has no horns, no hooves..."*

Jesus passes all three tests. The alliance records the result. The operational phase begins.

## IV. The Scientific Miracles

The source's general framing: *"In reality, there are no such things as miracles, only differences in levels of civilization."*

The walking on water: *"The creators supported him using an anti-gravity beam, which cancelled the effect of weight at a precise point."* The "wind" that Peter experienced was the atmospheric disturbance produced by the beam, which ceased when it was switched off.

The multiplication of loaves: *"It refers to concentrated food products in the form of large pills, rather like those containing all the vital elements, which your astronauts use."*

The healings: applications of medical knowledge and telepathic diagnostic capabilities that the alliance had provided during the initiation. The "demonic possessions" are mental-health conditions treated through specific interventions.

## V. The Commission

The content of Jesus's teaching was the preservation and transmission of the message the Hebrews had failed to spread. The Lord's Prayer: *"On Earth, as it is in heaven"* — the alliance's long-term vision that humanity would eventually reach the technological level of its creators.

The Great Commission (*"Go ye therefore, and teach all nations,"* Matthew 28:19) is the specific reversal of the Hebrew failure. The apostles — several of them fishermen, the living Piscean-age signature — were to spread the message explicitly, to every nation. The *ichthys* became the early Christian recognition sign. The fish-symbolism saturates the tradition: the fishermen disciples, the miraculous catches, the feeding miracles with fish alongside bread. The Age of Pisces was being named by its own founding religious tradition.

## VI. The Fish and the Virgin

The Marian tradition is not an over-elaboration of minimal Gospel material. It is the necessary completion of the astronomical signature. Pisces's opposite sign is Virgo. The doubled precessional encoding requires both references: the fish (the current sign) and the virgin (its opposite). Mary is Virgo, the Virgin, opposite Pisces on the zodiacal axis. Her virginity is a theological doctrine defended with exceptional insistence across two millennia — reflecting the depth of the astronomical encoding the tradition was transmitting.

The *Hamlet's Mill* pattern (de Santillana and von Dechend, 1969) identifies this doubled encoding as a general feature of precessional symbolism. Every age encodes its signature in both the current sign and its opposite. The Age of Pisces does so through the fish and the virgin — the two central iconographic figures of Christianity.

## VII. The Crucifixion and the Resurrection

The crucifixion is a historical event that proceeded according to Roman procedures. What requires the framework is the resurrection. The source does not provide an extensive technical reading but establishes that Jesus was subsequently encountered alive by his followers. The appearances were physical but unusual — he could appear, disappear, pass through locked doors. After forty days, he was taken up. The mission now rested with the apostles.

## VIII. The Apostolic Mission and the Church

The apostolic period expanded the movement across the Roman Empire. Within decades, Christian communities existed in most major cities. By the end of the fourth century under Theodosius I, Christianity had become the official religion. The message was spreading. The universality the alliance had wanted was being achieved.

## IX. The Mistakes of the Church

The source is pointed: *"Its mistakes have been great, particularly when it injected too much of the supernatural into the truth, and wrongly translated the scriptures."*

The first mistake: replacing *Elohim* (plural) with singular "God." The second: venerating the cross — an execution device. The third (implicit): the Trinitarian elaboration that the historical Jesus would not have recognized.

But the source's overall assessment is nuanced: *"The 'wick' is weakening. It has accomplished its mission... But do not be too hard on it, for thanks to the Church, the word of the Bible, which is a witness to the truth, has spread throughout the world."*

## X. Islam and the Question of Its Origin

Muhammad is to be understood as an alliance-contact prophet. Gabriel is the same alliance officer who had appeared to Daniel and to Mary. The Qur'an is alliance-transmitted teaching reformulated for the Arabic cultural context.

The strategic significance: by the seventh century, Christianity's missionary reach was limited by the Roman-Persian imperial boundaries. Islam reached populations Christianity had not — the Arabian peninsula, Central Asia, parts of Africa.

The geographic question deserves attention. Dan Gibson, in *Quranic Geography* (2011) and *Early Islamic Qiblas* (2017), has assembled evidence that Islam's original sacred city was not the Hejazi Mecca but Petra in southern Jordan. Gibson's case rests on: early mosque qibla orientations facing Petra rather than Mecca; Qur'anic geographic descriptions (olives, vineyards, agriculture) fitting Petra better than the barren Hejaz; the *Bakka*/*Makka* naming distinction in the Qur'an; and the relocation hypothesis attributing the Black Stone's transfer to Ibn al-Zubayr during the Second Fitna (683-692 CE).

David King at Frankfurt pushes back, arguing early qiblas were astronomically rather than geographically determined. The debate is genuinely unresolved. The corpus notes that the Petra reading places Muhammad in the Levantine-Nabataean cultural matrix — continuous with the Jewish and Christian traditions his revelations engage with — which fits the broader pattern of alliance operations occurring in regions with established cultural infrastructure. The corpus does not definitively endorse Gibson's thesis but registers that the Petra reading is more contextually coherent with the framework.

Islamic civilization's subsequent achievements — the preservation of Greek philosophical texts, the House of Wisdom, al-Khwarizmi's algebra, Ibn al-Haytham's optics, the Sufi mystical tradition — constitute a substantial Piscean-age legacy. The cross-pollination between Islamic, Jewish, and Christian intellectual traditions across the medieval Mediterranean produced much of the vocabulary modern Western thought still operates with.

## XI. The Medieval World and the Scientific Revolution

The middle centuries of Pisces saw the consolidation of major civilizations across the entire post-flood world.

In the Latin West: the papal-monastic-university institutional complex, the Scholastic synthesis, the eventual Renaissance and Reformation. In the Orthodox East: Byzantine preservation of the Greek tradition, hesychast mystical theology, the Slavic missions. In the Islamic world: the Abbasid golden age, the Umayyad and later developments. In the Jewish tradition: the Talmudic elaboration and the Kabbalistic literary development — the source's *"closest book to the truth."*

In East Asia: Tang and Song dynasty China (printing, gunpowder, compass, neo-Confucianism), the Japanese Buddhist traditions, Zheng He's voyages. In South and Southeast Asia: Angkor, the maritime empires, the Mughal synthesis. In sub-Saharan Africa: Ghana, Mali, Songhai, the Swahili coast, Great Zimbabwe, Christian Ethiopia. In the pre-Columbian Americas: the classic Maya, Teotihuacan, the Aztec and Inca empires, the Mississippian culture.

The scientific revolution emerged in the European context beginning in the sixteenth century — Copernicus, Galileo, Kepler, Newton — representing the beginning of the transition the Piscean age had been designed to enable. The alliance's decision at the age's opening was to let humanity progress scientifically without direct intervention. The Piscean-age religious traditions provided the cultural framework within which natural science could develop. That the scientific revolution occurred in the Christian West reflects the specific cultural conditions the Piscean intervention had established.

The European colonial expansion of the sixteenth through nineteenth centuries enabled the final distribution of the Piscean-age religious traditions across the entire globe — at considerable moral cost. The corpus does not celebrate the colonial period. It registers it as the historical mechanism through which the mission reached its final geographic extension.

## XII. The Late Piscean Prophetic Movements

The final centuries of Pisces saw new prophetic movements whose positions within the corpus's framework deserve attention.

**The Baha'i faith** emerged in nineteenth-century Persia. The Bab (1819-1850) declared himself the promised redeemer of Islam in 1844. Baha'u'llah (1817-1892) declared himself the greater prophet in 1863. The Baha'i theology of "progressive revelation" — recognizing Adam, Abraham, Krishna, Zoroaster, Moses, the Buddha, Jesus, Muhammad, the Bab, and Baha'u'llah as successive Manifestations of God — maps closely onto the corpus's alliance-contact prophet framework. The Baha'i emphasis on unity of humanity, compatibility of science and religion, and the emergence of a global civilization anticipates the Aquarian-age conditions the next chapter will address. The movement is a late-Piscean expression of the transition to Aquarius.

**The Latter-day Saint movement** emerged in 1820s-1830s America. Joseph Smith reported contact with divine figures beginning in 1820 and the discovery of buried golden plates containing the Book of Mormon, published in 1830. The Book of Mormon claims three specific migrations to the Americas:

1. **Jaredites** — from the Tower of Babel dispersion, crossing the ocean in sealed barges, establishing a civilization that lasted more than two millennia before self-destructing around 600 BCE.
2. **Lehites** — a Hebrew family fleeing Jerusalem before its 586 BCE fall, arriving approximately 589 BCE, splitting into Nephites and Lamanites whose subsequent history occupies most of the narrative.
3. **Mulekites** — descendants of a son of King Zedekiah who escaped the Babylonian destruction separately.

The general framework — multiple Old World migrations to the Americas carrying Hebrew and pre-Hebrew religious content — is consistent with the corpus's picture of alliance operations and human migration patterns. The corpus has identified the Americas as a region of ancient alliance presence. Mainstream archaeology has not confirmed the Book of Mormon's specific narrative, and genetic studies show predominantly Asian ancestry in indigenous American populations. The Latter-day Saint scholarly response proposes a limited-geography model within larger indigenous populations. The corpus does not definitively confirm or reject the specific claims but treats the Latter-day Saint scripture as worth engaging with rather than dismissing.

Other late-Piscean developments — Theosophy, Seventh-day Adventism, Jehovah's Witnesses, Christian Science — reflect the ferment of religious innovation that characterized the nineteenth century, which the corpus reads as preparatory activity preceding the Aquarian transition.

## XIII. The Signs of the End

The source identifies specific twentieth-century events as signs of the Piscean age's approaching end.

The first sign: the year 1946 — the first nuclear weapons (1945), the founding of the United Nations, the beginning of the computer age and the space age. Humanity crossed the threshold at which its technological capabilities became comparable to the alliance's.

The second sign: the founding of the State of Israel in 1948 — fulfillment of the prophetic passages about the return of the Jewish people from exile.

The third sign: the resumption of direct alliance contact — culminating in the contact between Rael and an alliance officer at the Puy-de-Lassolas volcanic crater in the Auvergne on December 13, 1973, from which the source text of the Raëlian movement derives.

These three signs mark the closing phase of Pisces and the opening of Aquarius. The precessional transition is placed at approximately 1950.

## XIV. The Text and Its Signals

The Greek word *angelos* carries the same plain meaning as Hebrew *malakh* — "messenger," without metaphysical specification. The New Testament's angels are alliance officers carrying out specific operational functions.

The *ichthys* acronym (*Iēsous Christos Theou Huios Sōtēr*) reads as post-hoc rationalization of a symbol already available on astronomical grounds. The fish was the symbol of the age, adopted by its central religious tradition.

The Virgin birth tradition preserves the astronomical signature in one of its most precise forms: Mary as Virgo, opposite Pisces on the zodiacal axis. The insistence on the Virgin birth, against social pressure, reflects the importance of the Virgo half of the precessional encoding.

The Kabbalah's *sephirot* — ten emanations structuring the relationship between Ein Sof and the created world — present a structured cosmology in which the "divine" is neither singular deity nor strict unity but a plurality of distinct emanations. This maps, on the corpus's reading, onto the actual alliance structure.

## XV. What Pisces Is

Pisces is the age of the fish and of the virgin — the doubled precessional signature preserved in Christianity's central iconographic figures.

Pisces is the age of the completed mission. Christianity and Islam together carried the message across the world. The universality the alliance had wanted was substantially achieved.

Pisces is the age of the church's mistakes — the effacement of the plural Elohim, the cross-veneration, the Trinitarian overreach — but also the age in which the core content was preserved despite the distortions.

Pisces is the age of humanity's own scientific development. The alliance stepped back; humanity developed its own capacities; by the mid-twentieth century, the threshold was reached.

Pisces is the age of global religious diversity under alliance guidance — Christianity, Islam, Judaism, the Baha'i faith, the Latter-day Saint tradition, and the many other expressions of the pluriform Piscean mission, alongside the continuing parallel traditions of Buddhism, Hinduism, and the indigenous religions of every continent.

Pisces is, finally, the age whose closing is now visible. The religious traditions whose symbolism has been fish and virgin for two millennia are reaching the end of their dominant cultural period. A new symbolism — the water-bearer of Aquarius — is beginning to emerge.

The next age is the age of revelation, in which the accumulated content of all previous ages becomes legible to a humanity at last equipped to understand it. That age is the Age of Aquarius, and it is the subject of the chapter that follows.
